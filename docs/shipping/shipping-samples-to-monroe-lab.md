# Shipping Frozen Samples to the Monroe Lab (Collaborator Guide)

**Purpose:** How to package and ship frozen biological samples on dry ice to the Monroe Lab at UC Davis using a prepaid shipping label that the lab generates for you. This is the collaborator-facing half — your contact at the Monroe Lab generates and emails you the label; you do the packing and the carrier handoff.

**Companion protocol (lab side):** [[shipping/aggieship-inbound-hazmat-label]] — what we do on the AggieShip side to generate the label.

---

## The whole thing in 5 bullets

- Email Grey one week before you want to ship: number of tubes, package weight (kg), box dimensions, dry ice amount (kg), your full ship-from address, and your intended ship date.
- Lab emails you a prepaid PDF shipping label.
- Pack samples in **plain cardboard** (NOT FedEx/UPS branded), with dry ice, double-bagged with absorbent material.
- Print and tape on the label, write the destination address on the box too as a backup.
- Hand to FedEx **Mon, Tue, or Wed only**, then email Grey the tracking number.

## Before you ship — at least one week ahead

Email `gmonroe@ucdavis.edu` with the following information:

| What | Why we need it | Example |
|---|---|---|
| Number of tubes/samples | Dept reference | `42 tubes` |
| Estimated total package weight (gross, including dry ice) | Required field on the AggieShip label, in kilograms | `4.5 kg` |
| Outer box dimensions (L × W × H) | Required field on the label, inches or cm | `12 × 10 × 10 in` |
| Approximate dry ice weight (kg) | Required hazmat declaration | `2.3 kg (5 lb)` |
| Your full ship-from address | Goes on the label as the origin | name, street, city, state, zip, country, phone, email |
| Your intended ship date (Mon, Tue, or Wed) | Goes on the label as the promised ship date | `2026-04-15` |

We'll generate a prepaid FedEx Priority Overnight label via UC Davis AggieShip and email you the PDF. Hazmat labels are reviewed by the AggieShip team before they go live, which can take 1 to 3 business days, so please don't leave the request to the day before you want to ship.

> **Why kilograms?** AggieShip's hazmat module requires kilograms for both gross weight and dry ice weight. Round generously upward — under-reporting weight or dry ice quantity can cause carrier rejection.

### Alternative: ship it yourself

If it's easier for you to use your own institution's carrier account and bill us back, that's totally fine. Save the receipt, follow the same packing protocol below, and email Grey the tracking number plus the receipt. We'll reimburse.

## What you'll need to pack the box

- **Plain cardboard outer box.** This is important: hazmat shipping regulations forbid FedEx, UPS, or DHL branded boxes and envelopes. Any unbranded sturdy cardboard box works.
- **Insulating cooler** (styrofoam shipper) sized to fit inside the cardboard outer box with room for dry ice
- **Dry ice.** Roughly 2 to 5 kg (5 to 10 lb) is typical for an overnight shipment; size up if your routing is longer or your local FedEx pickup time is early. Aim for enough dry ice to last 2× the actual transit time as a safety margin.
- **Watertight outer bag** (large Ziploc) for secondary containment
- **Absorbent material** (paper towels or absorbent pads) to go inside the secondary bag with the tubes
- **Permanent marker** with waterproof ink
- **Clear packing tape**
- **Two printouts**: the prepaid label PDF (full page, do not scale to fit) and your filled sample sheet

---

## Packing the box

### 1. Primary containment (the tubes)

Each tube is the primary container. Make sure caps are tight. Verify the labels are still readable; if any are smudged, re-label with permanent marker before packing.

### 2. Secondary containment

1. Place all the labeled tubes inside a large watertight Ziploc bag.
2. Add absorbent material (paper towels or absorbent pads) inside the bag — enough to soak up the contents of every tube if all of them somehow leaked. This is a hazmat regulation, not a suggestion.
3. Seal the Ziploc.

### 3. Insulation and dry ice

1. Place the sealed Ziploc inside the styrofoam shipper.
2. Add dry ice around the bag, filling the styrofoam shipper.
3. Place a printed copy of your filled sample sheet inside the styrofoam lid (so we have a hard copy in the box even if the outer label is damaged in transit).
4. Close the styrofoam shipper.

### 4. Outer box

1. Place the styrofoam shipper inside the plain cardboard outer box. Add packing material to fill any voids.
2. Tape the box closed with clear packing tape on all the seams.

> **Don't seal the box airtight.** Dry ice sublimes into CO2 gas as it warms. An airtight box will pressurize and can burst. Standard cardboard boxes and styrofoam shippers vent enough on their own, so don't go overboard with multiple layers of tape.

## Labeling the outside of the box

### 1. The prepaid shipping label

Print the PDF you received in **full size** (do NOT shrink-to-fit). Tape it flat to the top of the box with clear packing tape. **Do not let tape cover the barcode** — carrier scanners can't read through some tapes.

### 2. Address backup (in waterproof ink)

With permanent marker, write the destination address directly on the box as a backup in case the label is damaged:

```
Grey Monroe Lab

University of California, Davis
Davis, CA 95616
USA
Phone: 279-222-8881
```

### 3. Responsible party (in waterproof ink)

Write on the outside of the box:

```
Responsible party: Grey Monroe, 279-222-8881
```

This is required by hazmat regulations.

### 4. UN 1845 / dry ice diamond

The prepaid label from AggieShip will include the **UN 1845 dry ice hazmat diamond** and the **dry ice weight in kilograms**. Verify both are visible and legible before sealing the box. If anything looks wrong on the label, email Grey before you ship.

## Handing off to the carrier

- **Mon, Tue, or Wed only.** Thursday and Friday shipments can sit in a warehouse over the weekend, and your dry ice will be gone by Monday.
- **Hand to a human at a staffed FedEx (or whichever carrier the label specifies) location.** Hazmat shipments cannot be left at unattended dropboxes.
- Or, **call the carrier for a pickup at your lab.** The label is prepaid; you do not need a carrier account number of your own.
- After handoff, **email the tracking number to `gmonroe@ucdavis.edu` immediately** — we use this to make sure the box doesn't sit in our mailroom over a weekend.
- Also email the **filled sample sheet** (CSV or photo of the printed sheet) the same day.

## Troubleshooting

> *I'm out of dry ice and can't ship today.* Stop. Re-freeze the samples at −80°C if possible and email Grey to reschedule. Don't ship under-iced.

> *The label arrived but I can't ship until next week.* The label has a "Promised Date" on it. As long as you ship within a few days of that date the label is valid. If the delay is longer, email Grey so we can re-issue the label with an updated date.

> *Carrier rejected the package at the counter.* Don't argue. Bring it home, re-ice if needed, and email Grey with what they said. The most common reasons are a missing UN 1845 diamond, the wrong type of outer box (carrier-branded), or weight mismatch.

> *The shipping label PDF didn't arrive.* Check your spam filter, then email Grey directly. We can re-print and re-send.

> *I want to ship myself with my own institution's carrier.* That's fine — save the receipt, use the same packing protocol, and we'll reimburse. Email Grey the tracking number on ship day.

> *The tubes are all flash-frozen but I don't have a styrofoam shipper.* Email Grey before you collect samples, not after. We can ship you supplies in advance.
