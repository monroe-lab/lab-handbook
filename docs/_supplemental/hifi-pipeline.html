Nuclease Treatment — BIGGEST LOSS STEP
⏱ 30 min Recovery: 40-50% Nuclease mix (keep on ice!)
What it degradesWhat it preserves
Failed ligation products
Unligated linear DNA
Adapter dimers
Short junk fragments		Correctly-formed circular SMRTbell templates (capped ends resist exonuclease)
You're losing ~50% of material, but it's the bad half. These are molecules that would waste sequencing capacity. The nuclease preferentially digests short linear fragments while circular SMRTbell templates survive.
Why not skip this if you're doing size selection anyway? Nuclease removes biochemical junk (dimers, failed ligations). Size selection does physical size cutoff. Different purposes. Skipping cleanup would overload the size selection instrument and contaminate the size-selected fraction.
40-50% lost here
7
AMPure PB Bead Cleanup
⏱ 30 min Recovery: 70-80% 3.1X v/v beads (permissive ratio)
MetricValue
Bead ratio3.1X v/v (35% AMPure PB)
Wash2x 80% EtOH
Elution21 μL
QCQubit HS — dilute 1:10 before measuring!
Expected concentration16-30 ng/μL
Expected total340-640 ng
This is NOT precise size selection. The 3.1X ratio retains most fragments >1-2 kb. It's cleanup + coarse selection. Real size selection is next.
Lab experience (pistachio): 12 samples yielded 342-638 ng in 21 μL. Overall recovery from gDNA: 20-26%.
