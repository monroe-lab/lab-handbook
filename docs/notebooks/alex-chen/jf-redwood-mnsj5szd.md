# DNA Extraction Optimization - Sequoia sempervirens

**Date:** 2026-04-10
**Researcher:** James Freckles

## Objective

Compare standard (100uL) vs. reduced volume (50uL) elution using Qiagen DNeasy Plant Mini Kit for S. sempervirens leaf tissue. Hypothesis: 50uL elution yields higher concentration with minimal total yield loss.

## Sample Collection

Fresh leaf tissue collected from mature coast redwood (Sequoia sempervirens) at the UC Davis Arboretum, north grove. Young expanding leaves from lower canopy, \~200mg collected per sample. Tissue placed immediately in liquid nitrogen.

## Methods

1. Tissue grinding per lab grinding protocol (mortar + pestle, LN2, \~100mg per sample)
2. Sample A: Standard DNeasy protocol, 100uL AE buffer elution
3. Sample B: Modified DNeasy protocol, 50uL AE buffer elution
4. NanoDrop quantification of both samples (1uL each)
5. Gel electrophoresis: 0.8% agarose, 1X TAE, 100V, 30 min, 5uL per lane + 1uL 6X loading dye
