# Harvesting microshoot cultures

*Created by Grey Monroe on 2026-05-13*

## Purpose

This protocol describes how to harvest plant material from pistachio microshoot cultures while preserving the identity of individual plant sources within each culture container.
These microshoot cultures often contain multiple clusters of shoots. Each cluster originates from a single callus source, typically visible as a dark or black clump at the base of the shoots. Different clumps within the same container may represent different originating plant sources. The goal of this protocol is to avoid mixing material between clumps during harvesting.
This material will be used for downstream DNA sequencing and mutation analysis.
\-\-\-

## Important concept

The most important part of this protocol is preserving the identity of each shoot cluster.
\- Shoots sharing the same black callus mass are considered one source\.
\- Different black masses may represent different originating sources\.
\- Avoid mixing shoots from separate callus clumps\.
Sometimes the organization of the culture is ambiguous. Do not stress about making perfect decisions, but be thoughtful and consistent.
If uncertain:
\- Harvest material from only one side of a callus mass\.
\- Avoid combining material across visually separate clumps\.
If a single callus produces multiple shoots, those shoots can all be collected together.
\-\-\-

## Materials

* Pistachio microshoot cultures
* 15 mL Falcon tubes
* Forceps and scalpel
* Liquid nitrogen or access to a -80°C freezer
* Tray for organizing samples
* Camera or phone for documentation

\-\-\-

## Photo documentation

Before harvesting each culture container:T ake a clear photo of the culture container. Make sure the label is visible in the image.

> ℹ️ **Note**
> The purpose of the photo is to document culture condition and morphology before harvesting.
> This documentation may become important later if unusual sequencing results or mutations are identified. Poor growth, abnormal morphology, or unhealthy tissue could provide biologically meaningful context for downstream analyses.

## Harvesting guidelines

For each culture container:
1\. Identify visually distinct shoot clusters\.
2\. Locate the black callus mass associated with each cluster\.
3\. Carefully harvest green plant material from one individual clump source\.
4\. Try to collect the entire plantlet or connected group of shoots from that clump\.
5\. Avoid collecting media\.
6\. Avoid mixing material between separate clumps\.
7\. Place harvested material into the appropriate labeled 15 mL Falcon tube\.
Notes

> ⚠️ **Warning**
> A single harvested source may contain multiple small shoots if they originate from the same callus.
> If boundaries between sources are unclear, harvest conservatively from one side only.
> Prioritize preserving source identity over maximizing biomass.

\-\-\-

## Sample collection target

From each culture container:

* Harvest approximately five individual plant sources when possible.
* Each source should be collected separately.

\-\-\-

## Storage

Immediately after harvesting:
**Flash freeze samples in liquid nitrogen**
OR
**Place samples on dry ice and transfer to a -80°C freezer as quickly as possible.**
**Avoid allowing samples to thaw after collection.**
