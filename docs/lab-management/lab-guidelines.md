# Monroe Lab Guidelines

Monroe Lab Guide to Success: Cultivating a Thriving Research Environment

Welcome to the Monroe Lab! This guide outlines our shared values and expectations, ensuring a positive, productive, and respectful environment for everyone. We believe that a strong foundation of curiosity, collaboration, and communication is key to groundbreaking discoveries and personal growth.

# Our Core Values

## Curiosity

At the heart of scientific research is curiosity. We encourage a desire to learn, question, and explore - to embrace the journey of discovery and always seek to understand the "why" and "how."

## Collaboration

Great science is rarely done in isolation. We strive for a collaborative environment, where sharing ideas, expertise, and support strengthens our collective efforts. Work together, learn from each other, and celebrate our successes as a team.

## Communication

Open and honest communication is vital for a functional and healthy lab. We prioritize clear, timely, and constructive dialogue to ensure smooth operations, address concerns, and facilitate learning.

# Expectations for Lab Members

## Respect and Professionalism

Every member of the Monroe Lab is expected to treat each other with respect, professionalism, and kindness. We value diversity in thought and background, and we are committed to creating an inclusive environment where everyone feels safe and valued.

## Timely Communication

We understand that unexpected situations arise. If you are unable to attend pre-arranged meetings or scheduled lab times with your mentor, please communicate as quickly as possible. This allows us to adjust plans and ensure efficiency.

## Expressing Concerns Promptly

Your well-being and the integrity of our research are paramount. We encourage you to express any concerns quickly as they arise. This could be about:

- Safety: If you observe or suspect any safety hazards, please speak up immediately.
- Uncertainty over Expectations: If you are unsure about a task, protocol, or what is expected of you, please ask for clarification.
- Research Integrity: If you suspect a sample was mixed up, a procedure was done incorrectly, or any other issue that could impact our data, please share it. This is a learning environment where we all make mistakes – just ask anyone in the lab! You are not only expected to, but encouraged to, communicate if you think something went wrong.

Our priority is that you feel confident to learn and grow through experience in the lab. Openly addressing mistakes helps us learn and improve as a team.

## Ask Questions!

The more questions you ask, the better! Asking questions demonstrates your interest, your critical thinking, and your commitment to learning. There are no "silly" questions in our lab. We are here to support your development and help you deepen your understanding.

---

We are excited to have you as part of the Monroe Lab. By upholding these values and expectations, we all contribute to a dynamic, supportive, and successful research environment.
