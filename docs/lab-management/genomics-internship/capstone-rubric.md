# Capstone Presentation Rubric

Week 10. Lab meeting. 15-20 min talk + 10 min Q&A.

This is the capstone. Pass means tech-job or project-path offer; fail doesn't mean you didn't learn — but the offer is on the line.

## Required sections (all four)

### 1. Background — what your protein does

- What is the gene? (AGI locus, common name)
- What pathway is it in? (DNA damage repair — base excision, mismatch, double-strand break, etc.)
- What does the protein do mechanistically?
- What's known from prior published work? (Cite at least 3 papers.)
- What are the published mutant phenotypes for this gene or pathway?

### 2. Methods + your assembly results

- Your protocol path: extraction method, sequencing platform, assembly tool
- Genome stats: contig N50, total assembly size, number of contigs
- Where in the genome is your gene? Show the locus.
- Show the T-DNA insertion site visually (IGV or similar)
- If you pivoted to backup tissue (see [[contamination-escape]]), document that here

### 3. Hypotheses for the mutant phenotype

- Given the protein's function, what do you predict the mutant accumulates?
  - Specific mutation type? (transitions, transversions, indels, structural variants)
  - Specific genomic context? (CG / CHG / CHH, transcribed regions, etc.)
  - Specific rate increase compared to wild-type?
- Justify each hypothesis with mechanism + prior literature

### 4. Proposed next experiments

- What would you do with another quarter on this line?
- What additional data would you collect?
- What controls would you run?
- What's the most interesting follow-up?

## Format

- 15-20 min talk + 10 min Q&A
- Slides expected (Google Slides or PowerPoint)
- Submit slides to Kehan by end of Week 9 for review
- Practice run with Kehan or a lab-mate before lab meeting
- Lab meeting venue: Robbins 289, Tuesdays 10am-12pm

## Did-well criteria — all four required

These are the criteria for the **tech-job or project-path offer**:

- [ ] **Delivered a usable genome assembly** — clean, polished, T-DNA insertion locatable
- [ ] **Lab notebook is complete and well-documented** — daily entries throughout, every protocol cited, every gel/plate annotated, results tracked
- [ ] **Presentation demonstrates real understanding** — answers Q&A confidently, shows you understand mechanism not just procedure
- [ ] **Showed up reliably and worked independently** — attendance, initiative, you didn't need to be babysat through the program

Hit all four → you get the post-program offer.

Hit three → conversation with Grey about what's next; offer may still be on the table depending on circumstances.

Hit fewer than three → no offer, but the experience is still real, you have a genome you assembled, and the lab notebook is yours to keep.

## What grading looks like

Kehan + Grey grade against this rubric within a week of the presentation. Decisions communicated by end of week 11.
