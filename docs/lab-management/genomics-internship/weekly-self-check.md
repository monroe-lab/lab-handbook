# Weekly Self-Check

Each week, you answer 3-5 short questions about what you did and why. Write the answers in your lab notebook (1-2 sentences each is plenty).

This is **not a quiz with a grade.** It's a tool to make sure you can actually explain what you're doing — because by Week 10 you have to give a presentation on it. If you can't answer one of these, that's a flag to read up or ask Kehan before it compounds.

## Phase 0 — before Day 1

These are covered by the educational quiz. If you passed the quiz, you've got these.

- What is *Arabidopsis thaliana*? Why is it a good model organism?
- What does PCR do, and what's a thermocycler?
- What is DNA extraction, in three sentences?
- What are the major sequencing methods (Sanger, Illumina, Nanopore, PacBio HiFi) and what is each best for?
- What is a mutation? What's a T-DNA insertion specifically?
- What gene have you been assigned, and what does it do?

## Week 1

- What's the difference between a P10, P200, and P1000 pipette? Which would you use for 50 µL?
- What's the agar concentration in your seedling-planting agar solution, and why is it that low?
- What does sterilization remove from seeds, and what does it not remove?
- Why is the germination assay scored over multiple days instead of just at the end?
- What does it mean if a stock line has a low germination rate? Speculate.

## Week 2

- What are the ratios you check on the NanoDrop, and what does each indicate about contamination?
- What's the difference between Qubit and NanoDrop? When do you trust which?
- Why do we drill the HMW extraction on practice tissue before doing it on your own seedlings?
- What's the positive control in your T-DNA PCR practice run, and what's the negative?
- What was the germination rate variance across stock lines? What's a possible biological explanation?

## Week 3 (harvest week — busy, but answer briefly)

- Why 14-day seedlings specifically? What's the trade-off with younger or older?
- Why do we pool 50-100 seedlings instead of using just one?
- What does it mean if your T-DNA PCR shows a band in the wild-type-allele reaction?
- What did you do this week that you'd do differently next time?

## Weeks 4-7 — sequencing turnaround + assembly

- What's a contig? What's a scaffold? What's the difference?
- What is N50, and why do we care?
- What assembly tool are you running, and what does it actually do under the hood (in plain English)?
- Where in the genome is your gene?
- Can you see the T-DNA insertion in your assembly? What does the insertion site look like?

## Weeks 8-9 — presentation prep

- What three papers are the most important on your gene, and why each?
- What mutant phenotypes have been published for your gene or its pathway?
- Given the protein's function, what kinds of mutations do you predict accumulate in this MA line?
- What experiment would actually test that hypothesis?
- What's the strongest objection to your hypothesis? How would you address it?

## How to use this

- Pick a regular slot — Friday afternoon is a good one — to fill in that week's questions
- 1-2 sentences each is plenty; this is not an essay
- Bring the answers to your weekly lab-meeting check-in if you want feedback
- These answers are the rough draft of your week-10 presentation. By Week 9 you should be able to assemble a talk just from these notes.

## If you can't answer one

Don't panic; this is the point. Two options:

1. Read about it. Lab handbook → search the topic. If it's a published-literature question, check Google Scholar.
2. Ask Kehan or a lab-mate. The point of weekly check-ins is to surface this stuff while it's still fixable.
