# Monroe Lab Genomics Internship

Hands-on internship in plant genomics. By the end of one quarter, every student delivers a polished long-read genome assembly of a DNA-repair-deficient *Arabidopsis* mutation-accumulation line — and a lab-meeting talk on what their gene does and why we care.

## The science you're doing

Each student is assigned one **DNA-repair-deficient mutant line** (a SALK T-DNA insertion that disrupts a specific repair gene). The line has been propagated through a mutation-accumulation regime — each generation passes through a single-seed bottleneck, so spontaneous mutations build up unimpeded. Without that repair pathway, the line should accumulate a particular *kind* of mutation at a particular *rate*.

Your job: assemble that line's genome, find your gene, find the T-DNA insertion, and lay the groundwork for the lab to call mutation spectra against the reference.

## Two paths after the program

Students who finish well are offered one of two next steps:

- **Lab technician** — paid position, broad lab support work
- **Independent project / technique development** — own a research direction or new method

"Finish well" means all four of these:

1. Delivered a usable genome assembly
2. Lab notebook complete and well-documented
3. Lab-meeting presentation demonstrates real understanding of your gene
4. Showed up reliably, worked independently

## Timeline at a glance

| When | What |
|---|---|
| **Phase 0** (~5 wk before, variable) | EHS, keys, greenhouse training, Kehan safety, lit review on your gene, week-1 protocol pre-read, educational quiz |
| **Week 1** | Pipette school, agar prep, plant in greenhouse, plate germination assay |
| **Week 2** | Score germination assay, drill DNA extraction on bulk practice tissue |
| **Week 3** | **Harvest 14-day seedlings → HMW → T-DNA PCR → library prep → submit sequencing by Friday** |
| **Weeks 4-7** | Sequencing turnaround, assembly, lab notebook polishing |
| **Weeks 8-9** | Presentation prep, lit review deepening |
| **Week 10** | Lab-meeting capstone presentation |

## Week 1 — get plants in, get going

- **Day 1:** [[pipette-school]], make [[agar-solution-low-density]], plant your line in the greenhouse, plate the germination assay across all stock lines, scope your lab notebook
- **Days 2-5:** score early germination, run [[quick-dna-extraction]] → [[qubit-quantification]] → one HMW extraction on bulk frozen practice tissue Kehan provides
- **Off-bench (~3 hr):** read week 2-3 protocols, continue lit review on your assigned gene

## Week 2 — drill so it works on harvest day

- Score germination assay → **% germinated per stock line** (a fitness readout — low germ may mean a deleterious mutation), annotate plate photos, run a basic stats comparison across lines
- Repeat HMW extraction on practice tissue until reproducible
- T-DNA PCR practice run with your stock primers on positive/negative control DNA
- Off-bench: deepen lit review, draft presentation outline

## Week 3 — harvest and beeline

| Day | What |
|---|---|
| Mon (day 14) | Harvest 50-100 seedlings, flash-freeze in LN2 |
| Mon-Tue | HMW extraction on your own material |
| Wed | Qubit + NanoDrop QC |
| Wed-Thu | T-DNA PCR with stock primers, gel |
| Thu-Fri | If clean → HiFi/Nanopore library prep |
| **Fri** | **Sequencing submitted** |

If T-DNA PCR shows wild-type contamination → see [[contamination-escape]]. Pivot to pre-staged backup tissue, no time lost.

## Weeks 4-7 — sequencing turnaround + assembly

- Nanopore drives the timeline; PacBio HiFi is bonus pass if budget/slots allow
- Data back → [[genome-assembly-module]] (a one-line run on the lab Mac, no farm access needed)
- Polish, find your T-DNA insertion in the assembly, visualize at the locus
- Off-bench: deepen lit review on your gene, draft methods section

## Weeks 8-9 — presentation prep

- Annotate assembly results
- Build slides for week 10
- Practice talk with Kehan or a lab-mate

## Week 10 — capstone presentation

15-20 min talk + 10 min Q&A at lab meeting. Four required sections:

1. **Background** — what your protein does, mechanism, prior literature
2. **Methods + results** — your protocol path, genome stats, gene model at the T-DNA locus
3. **Hypotheses** — what you predict the mutant phenotype is, why
4. **Proposed next experiments** — what you'd do with another quarter

See [[capstone-rubric]] for the full rubric.

## Cohort + mentorship

- 3-4 students per quarter, every quarter
- One unique line per student
- **Kehan** is program TA — handles Day 1 onboarding, daily lab questions, weekly check-ins
- **Grey** directs science, joins milestone weeks (3, 7, 10), reviews presentations
- Group lab-meeting check-ins only — no scheduled 1:1s

## What you write down

**Lab notebook: one dated entry per lab session.** Cite the protocol used. Log observations. Photograph and annotate every gel, every plate. The lab notebook is what makes your week-10 presentation possible — and it's the gate for the post-program offer.

## Live review

Every week answers a few short self-check questions about what you did and why — see [[weekly-self-check]]. Builds toward the presentation, surfaces gaps in real time.
