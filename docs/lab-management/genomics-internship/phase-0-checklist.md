# Phase 0 Checklist — Before Day 1

Phase 0 is on **you**, the student. Lab gives you the checklist; pace is yours. Rule: **you don't start the program until everything below is checked**. If you dilly-dally, you don't show up Day 1.

Realistic minimum: ~4-5 weeks ahead. Greenhouse training is the long pole — slots fill far in advance, schedule first.

## Step 1: Express interest

- [ ] Submit the [Phase 0 application form](#) (link TBD). 5-10 questions: name, year, major, prior lab experience, why interested, time commitment confirmation.
- [ ] Wait for Grey to review and respond.

## Step 2: Get your line assignment

Once Grey accepts you, he'll assign you a **DNA-repair-deficient mutant line** (a SALK T-DNA insertion in a specific repair gene). Everything else in Phase 0 references this gene.

- [ ] Receive line assignment from Grey
- [ ] Confirm the gene name, AGI locus, SALK line ID, and the repair pathway it's in

## Step 3: Schedule greenhouse training (DO THIS FIRST — long pole)

UCD greenhouse access requires a training session. Slots fill weeks ahead. Schedule this **before anything else**.

- [ ] Visit the [UCD greenhouse training page](#) (link TBD — confirm with Grey)
- [ ] Find an open slot at least 2 weeks before your planned Day 1
- [ ] Register; add to your calendar
- [ ] Attend the training; receive greenhouse access

## Step 8: Lit review on your assigned gene

This is ongoing — start now, deepen all the way through Week 10. Goal: by your capstone presentation, you can explain what your protein does and what's known about the mutant phenotype.

Starter targets:

- [ ] Read the gene's TAIR / UniProt / NCBI page — what does the protein do
- [ ] Find 3-5 key papers on this gene or its repair pathway
- [ ] Note any known mutant phenotypes
- [ ] Note any known mutation-spectrum signatures associated with this pathway
- [ ] Start a notes doc — this becomes your presentation Background section

## Step 10: Pass the educational quiz

A short quiz on the lab handbook covering:

- What is *Arabidopsis thaliana* and why is it a model
- What does PCR do (and what's a thermocycler)
- What is DNA extraction (cell lysis → release → purify)
- What sequencing methods exist (Sanger, Illumina, Nanopore, PacBio HiFi) and what each is good for
- What is a mutation; what's a T-DNA insertion specifically

- [ ] Take the quiz at [/internship/quiz/](#) (UI build pending)
- [ ] Pass with 80%+
