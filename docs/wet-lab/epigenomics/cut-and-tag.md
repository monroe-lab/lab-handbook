Nuclei Extraction Protocol [[1](https://link.springer.com/protocol/10.1007/978-1-4939-7318-7_12%23Sec9)]

\*use young tissue for best results.

Materials (per 1 sample)

- Mortar & pestle
- 3 x 50mL falcon tubes
- 5 x 1.5mL tubes (1 for NEB2, 2 per NEB3 round)
- 70um and 30/40 um filters (cell strainer)
- Liquid nitrogen, ice
- Buffers (see above)

Pre-cool the centrifuge, rotors, mortar & pestle, filters, and buffers prior to starting.

1. Collect plant samples and drop into liquid nitrogen (can use tinfoil or a tube).
2. Grind tissue (0.01- 0.2g) wth LN in a mortar, until it becomes a fine light-colored powder. Continue to add LN as grinding to prevent tissue from defrosting.
3. Add the ground tissue into a 50ml falcon tube containing 10mL NPB. Invert to homogenize tissue with a buffer. Filter through a 70um followed by a 30/40um filter using 50ml falcon tube to collect,  then centrifuge at 1200 xg at 4°C for 10 minutes.
4. Discard supernatant then add 1mL of NEB2 buffer and resuspend the pellet gently using a cut tip. Transfer into a 1.5mL tube.
5. Centrifuge 12000 xg at 4°C for 10 minutes. Meanwhile, add 400ul of NEB3 into a new 2ml tube.
6. Discard the supernatant and resuspend in 400ul NEB3 using a cut tip. Transfer the resuspended nuclei on top the layer of NEB3 in the new tube.
7. Centrifuge 12000 xg at 4°C for 10 minutes.
8. Repeat steps 6-8 if the pellet is still green. It should look white-gray.
9. Discard supernatant and add 1 ml NPB buffer and resuspend gently using a cut tip. The nuclei will fall to the bottom of the tube.

Count the nuclei on the CellDrop:

1. Dilute aliquots of the sample to 1:4 and 1:8 in NPB or H2O. Add equal volume of AO/PI dye and proceed to quantitation. No need to incubate.
2. When measuring the nuclei, make sure to adjust to the proper focus and avoid over-exposure.

\*To deal with light exposure, place a box around the reader when running  measurements.

Load Tn5 with adapters:

Tn5 Assembly Protocol [[2](https://www.protocols.io/view/bench-top-cut-amp-tag-kqdg34qdpl25/v3?step=1)]:

1. Anneal each of Mosaic end - adapter A (ME-A) and Mosaic end - adapter B (ME-B) oligonucleotides with Mosaic end – reverse oligonucleotides.
2. To anneal oligos, dilute oligonucleotides to 200 µM in annealing buffer (10mM Tris pH8, 50mM NaCl, 1 mM EDTA). Each pair of oligos, ME-A+ME-Reverse and ME-B+ME-Reverse, is mixed separately resulting in 100 µM annealed product.
3. Place the tubes in a 90-95°C hot block and leave for 3-5 minutes, then remove the hot block from the heat source allowing for slow cooling to RT (~45 minutes).
4. Mix 8 µL 100 µM pre-annealed ME-A and 8 µL 100 µM pre-annealed ME-B with 100 µL 5.5 µM pA-Tn5.
5. Incubate the mixture on a rotating platform for 1 hour at RT and then store at -20 °C.

Note about Tn5 stocks: As the purified Tn5 stocks contain traces of E.coli DNA, the loaded Tn5 can slowly tagment the E.coli DNA even if stored at -20C. It is recommended that the loaded Tn5 stocks not be stored/used for more than 6 months [[3](https://www.biorxiv.org/content/10.1101/2024.07.29.604300v1)].

CUT&Tag

Con-A Bead Activation

1. Fully resuspend Con-A beads (Epicypher CUTANA) by gently pipetting/vortexing. Transfer 11ul per reaction to 1.5ml tube. (10ul of the Con-A beads is sufficient for a single CUT&Tag reaction of up to 500,000 nuclei.)
2. Place the tube on a magnetic rack and allow the slurry to clear. Remove the supernatant. Move quickly to the next step to avoid bead dry-out.
3. Remove tube from the rack and immediately add 100ul per reaction cold Binding Buffer. Pipette to resuspend. Return tube to magnet to clear and remove supernatant.
4. Repeat the previous step for a total of 2 washes.
5. Resuspend the beads in 11ul per reaction cold binding buffer. Place on ice.

Bind nuclei to Con-A Beads

1. Aliquot x number of nuclei for a single CUT&Tag reaction into a clean 1.5ml tube.

100,000 nuclei + 10% to account for losses during washes

2. Centrifuge nuclei sample at 1200 xg at 4°C for 4 minutes. Try to remove as much of the supernatant without disturbing the nuclei pellet.
3. Resuspend the nuclei in 100ul of wash buffer. Add 10ul of the activated beads to the nuclei and pipette to mix. Incubate the mixture at RT for 10  minutes.
4. Place on the magnetic rack to clear. Aliquot 5ul of the supernatant into a new tube (this is the unbound fraction- keep on ice until use).
5. Remove the supernatant, taking care not to aspirate any beads out. Take the sample off the magnet and resuspend in 105ul of cold antibody buffer using a cut tip. Aliquot 5ul of the bead-nuclei solution into a  new tube (this is the bound fraction).
6. Add equal volume of AO/PI dye to the aliquots and run the unbound and/or bound fraction under the celldrop to confirm nuclei are successfully bound to the beads.

Note: The protocol below is written for centrifugation of nuclei during wash steps. If using Con-A beads, resume at step 5 below. For the washes, replace the centrifugation steps with clearing on the magnetic stand. Be cautious not to let the beads dry out during washing steps. Use cut tips for all the washing and resuspending steps until tagmentation is complete.

Prepare nuclei samples & Primary antibody incubation

1. Aliquot x number of nuclei for a single CUT&Tag reaction into a clean 1.5ml tube.

100,000 nuclei + 10% to account for losses during washes

2. Centrifuge nuclei sample at 1200 xg at 4°C for 4 minutes.
3. Remove the supernatant and wash with 500ul of ice-cold Wash Buffer. Centrifuge at 1200 xg for 4 min at 4°C. Discard the supernatant.
4. Remove the supernatant and resuspend the nuclei pellet in 100ul of antibody buffer.
5. Add 1ug of primary antibody to CUT&Tag treatment samples (2ul of 0.5mg/ml antibody; for IgG control add 1ul). Leave the other control samples as they are. Gently vortex the tubes to mix.
6. Incubate all treatment samples at 4°C overnight rocking on a nutator. Ensure that the nuclei don’t dry out/are suspended in solution by elevating tube caps on the nutator with tape.

Secondary antibody incubation

1. Centrifuge samples at 1200 xg at 4°C for 4 minutes.
2. Remove the supernatant and wash the nuclei pellet with 800ul ice-cold Wash Buffer. Centrifuge at 1200 xg at 4°C for 4 min. Discard supernatant. Repeat the wash step.
3. Dilute the secondary antibody 1:100 in Wash Buffer. Resuspend the nuclei pellet in 100ul of the diluted antibody by pipetting with cut tips. Resuspend control samples in 100ul Wash Buffer. Gently vortex tubes to mix.
4. Incubate at 4°C for 1h on a rocking on a nutator.

Bind transposome to nuclei

1. Centrifuge samples at 1200 xg at 4°C for 4 minutes.
2. Remove the supernatant and wash the nuclei pellets with 800ul ice-cold Transposase Incubation Buffer. Centrifuge at 1200 xg at 4°C for 4 min. Discard supernatant. Repeat the wash step.
3. Add 50ul Transposase Incubation Buffer to each reaction and resuspend by pipetting and/or gentle vortexing, followed by a quick spin.
4. Quickly spin the pAG-Tn5 stock (Epicypher CUTANA) and flick to mix. Add 2.5 µL pAG-Tn5 to each reaction. Gently vortex to evenly distribute.

1. 1ul for  pA-Tn5 from the active motif CUT&Tag kit.

5. Incubate at 4°C for 1h on a rocking on a nutator.

Tagmentation

1. Centrifuge samples at 1200 xg for 4 min at 4°C.
2. Remove the supernatant and wash the nuclei pellets with 800ul ice-cold Transposase Incubation Buffer. Centrifuge samples at 1200 xg for 4 min at 4°C. Repeat the wash step.
3. Mix 300 μl Transposase Incubation Buffer and 3 μl MgCl2 together (=9.9mM) and resuspend the nuclei pellet.
4. Incubate at 37°C at 500 rpm for 1h in the thermomixer.

STOP Tagmentation & DNA Extraction

1. Immediately after 1 hour of tagmentation at 37C, to quench tagmentation, add 10ul EDTA (0.5M), 3ul SDS (10%) and 2.5ul Proteinase K (20mg/ml). Incubate at 55°C  for 1h at 650 rpm to stop tagmentation and release DNA.
2. Spin phase-lock gel tubes at 16,000 xg for 2min at RT.

In the fume hood.

3. To each sample, add an equal volume (~300ul) of phenol-[[chloroform]]-isoamyl alcohol (pH 7.9) to the tagmentation product. Vortex or pipette well until the solution becomes cloudy.
4. Transfer the solution from step 3 to the centrifuged phase-lock gel tubes (optional: incubate (in the fume hood) for 10 minutes). It is okay that the solution separates during incubation.
5. Centrifuge at 16,000 xg for 5 min at RT.
6. Transfer the upper aqueous phase above the gel matrix to fresh 1.5ml tubes. Add 30ul 3M sodium acetate (pH 5.5), 2ul GlycoBlue, and 330ul cold isopropanol and vortex to mix well.
7. Incubate at −20°C overnight or -80 °C for at least 30 minutes.
8. Centrifuge at 16,000 g for 20 min at 4°C.
9. Wash the pellet twice with 1ml ice-cold 75% ethanol.
10. Air-dry the DNA pellet and dissolve the DNA in >=20 μl QIAGEN Buffer EB.
11. Quantitate DNA using Qubit HS dsDNA assay.

Library Cleanup / Size-Selection

Equilibrate AMPure XP beads to room temperature before use.

Prepare fresh 80% ethanol and place on ice.

Bead Ratio for 200 bp - 1.3 kb size selection of 50ul DNA volume:

0.75 - 0.5

R1 = 25 ul

R2 = 12.5 ul

1. Vortex the AMPure XP beads well. Add R1 volume (25ul) of the beads to the 50ul samples. Mix by pipetting >10x or vortexing and incubate at RT for 1 min.
2. Place the tubes onto a magnetic rack and allow beads to settle.
3. Transfer the clear supernatant (contain fragments <1300bp) to new tubes. Take care not to aspirate the beads.
4. Add R2 volume (12.5ul) of the beads to the supernatant from step 5. Mix well by pipette or vortexing. Incubate at RT for 1 min.
5. Place tubes onto a magnetic rack and allow beads to settle.
6. Remove and discard the clear supernatant. Take care not to aspirate the beads.
7. With the tubes still on the magnet, add 180ul of 80% ethanol and incubate at RT for 30sec-1min. Remove and discard the ethanol supernatant. Repeat the wash step.
8. To elute the sample:

1. Make sure the ethanol has evaporated (just until the beads turn matte, but not cracked).
2. Remove the tubes from the magnet and add >= 20ul of EB or NF H2O. Pipette to resuspend the beads well. Incubate at RT for 5 minutes.
3. Return tubes to the magnet and allow beads to settle.
4. Transfer the eluate to new labelled tubes.

Library size distribution considerations:

- CUT&Tag inserts adapters on either side of chromatin particles in the vicinity of the tethered enzyme, although tagmentation within chromatin particles can also occur. So, CUT&Tag reactions targeting a histone modification predominantly result in fragments that are nucleosomal lengths or multiples of that length (~147bp) CUT&Tag targeting transcription factors predominantly produce nucleosome-sized fragments and variable amounts of shorter fragments, from neighboring nucleosomes and the factor-bound site, respectively. Tagmentation of DNA on the surface of nucleosomes also occurs, and plotting fragment lengths with single-base pair resolution reveal a 10-bp sawtooth periodicity, which is typical of successful CUT&Tag experiments [[4](https://www.protocols.io/view/cut-amp-tag-data-processing-and-analysis-tutorial-e6nvw93x7gmk/v1?step=12.3)].

- i.e. on an [[agarose]] gel you should be able to see a ladder-like / banding pattern of multiples of nucleosome-size fragments; see examples: [[3](https://www.biorxiv.org/content/10.1101/2024.07.29.604300v1),[5](https://www.biorxiv.org/content/10.1101/2025.01.23.634238v1.abstract)]

- Note for reference: some [protocols](https://www.cellsignal.com/learn-and-support/technical-support/do-you-recommend-performing-size-selection-of-the-dna-during-the-library-preparation-for-cut-tag-experiments/000002053?srsltid=AfmBOopK8UMbK8WTk_rNJC5nzYxJ0PxUbcXxdC7-a7vWe-4Rh3aKeubv) omit the size-selection step.

- “Do you recommend performing size selection of the DNA during the library preparation for CUT&Tag experiments? No, we don't recommend performing size selection during the library purification for CUT&Tag. Based on our experience and feedback from customers, size selection can significantly decrease both the yield and diversity of the DNA library. This is extremely detrimental for CUT&Tag libraries due to the typical yield of the CUT&Tag assay. If you are concerned that large background DNA fragments (> 1 kb) will negatively impact next-generation sequencing (NGS), then simply decrease the extension time when amplifying the tagmented DNA during library preparation. We find that reducing the extension time to 10-15 seconds greatly reduces the amplification of large fragments (> 1kb), while still being sufficient for the amplification of smaller and more desirable DNA fragments during the CUT&Tag library preparation.”

- Nucleosomes are composed of approximately 147 bases of DNA wrapped around a histone octamer + adapters ~> 200 bp for mono-nucleosome fragments
- In order to capture inter-, mono, di-, tri- nucleosomal fragment lengths, size select for 0.2-1kb
