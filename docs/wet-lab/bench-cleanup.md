# Bench Cleanup Protocol

## Resources

**Supplies:** [[ethanol-70]] spray bottle, paper towels or lint-free wipes, waste containers

**Related Protocols:** [[lab-orientation]], [[waste-disposal-quick-reference]], [[spill-response]]

**Purpose:** The standard cleanup procedure after every bench session. This is not optional. You clean your bench after every use, every time, no exceptions.

## Time estimate

**Wall time:** ~5-10 min | **Hands-on:** 5-10 min

---

## When to Clean

- **After every protocol.** When you finish a protocol and are leaving the bench, clean it.
- **Before starting work** if the bench looks dirty or you're unsure about the previous user.
- **After any spill**, even small ones. See [[spill-response]] for hazardous spills.

## Procedure

### 1. Clear the bench

- Return all reagent bottles to their correct storage locations (chemical cabinets, fridge, freezer).
- Return shared equipment (pipettes, racks, timers) to their designated spots.
- Cap all tubes. Dispose of any empty or unneeded tubes.

### 2. Dispose of waste properly

| Waste | Where it goes |
|-------|--------------|
| Used pipette tips | Regular lab waste (if non-hazardous) or chemical waste (if contaminated with solvents, toxic reagents) |
| Used gloves | Regular lab waste |
| Gel waste ([[agarose]] + [[sybr-gold]]) | Check with Grey. SYBR Gold is a nucleic acid stain. |
| Empty chemical containers | Rinse 3x, then regular trash. Labels stay on. |
| Sharps (razor blades, broken glass, needles) | Sharps container. See [[sharps-handling]] |
| Liquid chemical waste | Labeled waste bottle under the fume hood. See [[waste-disposal-quick-reference]] |

### 3. Wipe down the surface

1. Spray the bench surface with [[ethanol-70|70% ethanol]].
2. Wipe with paper towels or lint-free wipes.
3. Wipe in one direction (toward you), not in circles. Circular wiping just moves contamination around.
4. For DNA work, this also reduces cross-contamination between experiments.

### 4. Check the floor

- If you dropped anything (tips, tube caps, paper), pick it up.

### 5. Wash your hands

- Remove gloves, dispose of them, wash your hands with soap and water before leaving the lab.

## Clean Bench (Laminar Flow Hood) Cleanup

The [[clean-bench-laminar-flow|laminar flow clean bench]] has its own cleanup procedure. See [[clean-bench]] for details. The short version:

1. Remove all materials from the bench.
2. Spray and wipe all surfaces inside the hood with 70% ethanol.
3. Run UV for 15 minutes (if the bench has a UV lamp).
4. Turn off the blower when done.

## Documentation

No lab notebook entry needed for routine cleanup. But if you find a mess that isn't yours, or if something looks wrong (unlabeled containers, unknown liquids, broken equipment), take a photo and notify Grey.
