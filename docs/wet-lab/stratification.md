# Stratification

**Purpose:** Cold treatment to break seed dormancy and synchronize germination.

---

## Background

Arabidopsis seeds have a dormancy mechanism that prevents germination immediately after maturation — in nature, this ensures seeds don't sprout in late summer only to die in winter. Stratification (cold + dark exposure) mimics the passage of winter and signals to the seed that it's safe to germinate.

**What's happening biologically:** During cold imbibition, gibberellic acid (GA) biosynthesis genes are upregulated and abscisic acid (ABA) — the dormancy-maintaining hormone — is degraded. After 2-4 days at 4 C, the GA/ABA ratio shifts enough that germination proceeds rapidly and uniformly upon transfer to warmth and light.

**Why it matters for experiments:** Without stratification, seeds germinate asynchronously over days to weeks. For controlled experiments where you need seedlings at the same developmental stage (especially for crossing, where you need flowers at the same time), synchronized germination is essential.

**On plates vs. in tubes:** You can stratify either way:
- **In tubes** (sterilized seeds in water, wrapped in foil, in fridge): Flexible — you can plate on different days from the same stratified batch.
- **On plates** (sow seeds, seal plates, put in fridge): One less handling step but commits you to a specific plating date.

---

## Procedure

1. After [[wet-lab/seed-sterilization|sterilizing seeds]], resuspend in sterile water (or leave in sterilization rinse water)
2. Wrap tubes in **aluminum foil** (dark + cold mimics winter)
3. Place at **4 C** (lab fridge) for **2 days** (48 hours)
4. After stratification, seeds are primed and will germinate uniformly when moved to light/warmth

> *Germination is staggered even after stratification:* Some ecotypes have stronger dormancy. Try extending to 3-4 days at 4 C. Kar-1 (Central Asian, cold climate origin) should stratify normally with 2 days.

> *Seeds germinated in the fridge:* The fridge may be too warm (check temperature), or seeds were exposed to light. Ensure tubes are wrapped in foil and fridge is at 4 C, not 8-10 C.
