# Protein Induction (BL21)

Soya Feb 2025

[protocol] protein induction using BL21

## Material

Day 1 & 2

- BL21 cells transformed with the expression vector
- 5 ml of LB for preculture
- 100 ml LB in 200 ml flask
- 1M IPTG stock (maize freezer/protein stuff box)
- Antibiotics

Day 3

- SDS Sample Buffer (maize freezer/protein stuff box). Supplement with DTT by following the instructions written on the protein stuff box.
- SDS-PAGE gel

We have a convenient  TGX Stain-free FastCast gel solution in the lab- it will make 10 % gel. If your protein needs thicker or thinner gel, we have everything needed for it too.

- 10 % APS stock (maize freezer/protein stuff box)

The most common reason for the gel not setting is expired APS. If the APS stock is more than 3 months old I would recommend making a fresh stock. APS powder is on Jiani’s bench.

- TEMED (Jiani’s bench)
- TGX Stain-free FastCast gel solution. (Jiani’s bench, with instruction)

- X1 Running Buffer (500 ml)

An empty bottle for x1 Running buffer is on Jiani’s bench. Make this on the day you run the gel.

- Mix 50 ml of x10 Running Buffer (Jiani’s bench), 5 ml of 10 % SDS, and 445 ml of DI water

- Protein ladder marker (maize freezer/protein stuff box)

## Notes

- Addition of IPTG will induce the protein expression (assuming your expression is lacO based)
- Add IPTG when OD is in the range of 0.4 to 0.8
- Effective working concentration for IPTG is 0.2-1 mM.
- IPTG powder is hydrophilic. Bring it to room temperature before measuring weight.
- Reduce the temperature during induction, typically 12-20 ℃. Some protein folds well in low temperatures.
- 100 ml of LB will be cultured, because the induction is more stable at larger volume, even though only 1 ml of the resulting culture will be used for SDS-PAGE.
- Because the migration of the prestained ladder protein is affected by the conjugated pigment, their size indication is not precise. Don’t worry too much if your protein size seems a few kDa off relative to the prestained ladder. If you need the absolutely precise ladder, buy an unstained ladder.

## Procedure

Day1

- Preculture your BL21 cells in 2~5 ml of LB + antibiotics

Day2

- Put 1 ml of LB to cuvette as a blank
- Add 100 µl of preculture to the 100 ml of LB + antibiotics, incubate @37℃, 120 rpm
- After 3 hours of culture, check the OD every 30 min.
- When the OD is between 0.4-0.8,

- sample 1 ml of the culture as a pre-induction control. Spindown to pellet the cell, remove the supernatant and freeze.
- add 50 µl of 1M IPTG/100 ml culture
- Reduce the temperature to 18℃, 120 rpm, culture overnight

Day3

- Sample 1 ml of culture, spindown to pellet the cell, remove the supernatant.
- Add 100 µl of x 1 SDS Sample Buffer to pre/post induction cell pellet, incubate at 95 ℃ for 15 min.
- Load samples to the SDS-PAGE gel. Preinduction = 8 µl, post-induction = 4 µl works. For the ladder, load 3 µl.
- Set the gel and run at 250 V for 30 min or until the dye front reaches the bottom of the gel.
- Remove the gel from the glass sandwich
- When you are using Stain-free gel, simply place your gel on the UV illuminator, wait for a few minutes for the signal to develop. Otherwise, stain with CBB (Jiani’s bench).
