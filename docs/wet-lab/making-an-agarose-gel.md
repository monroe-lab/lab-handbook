# Making an Agarose Gel

## Resources

**Equipment:** [[top-loading-balance]], microwave or hot plate, [[gel-electrophoresis-tank|gel casting tray]], combs, Erlenmeyer flask

**Reagents:** [[agarose]] (SeaKem LE or equivalent), 1x [[tae-buffer-50x|TAE buffer]]

**Related Protocols:** [[make-tae-buffer]], [[gel-electrophoresis]], [[gel-imaging-and-annotation]], [[how-to-use-the-scale]]

**Prerequisites:** [[make-tae-buffer]], [[how-to-use-the-scale]]

**Purpose:** Cast an agarose gel for DNA electrophoresis. This is a standalone protocol because you may need to cast gels for multiple protocols (PCR genotyping, extraction QC, library prep QC).

## Time estimate

**Wall time:** ~45 min (mostly cooling) | **Hands-on:** ~15 min

---

## Before You Start

Check that you have:

- [[agarose]] powder
- 1x [[tae-buffer-50x|TAE buffer]] (see [[make-tae-buffer]]; dilute 50x stock before use)
- A clean gel casting tray and comb(s)

## Background

Agarose is a polysaccharide extracted from seaweed. When dissolved in hot buffer and cooled, it forms a porous gel matrix. DNA fragments migrate through this matrix under an electric field, separating by size: smaller fragments move faster, larger fragments move slower. The gel percentage determines the pore size and optimal separation range.

### Choosing gel percentage

| Agarose % | Separation range | Use for |
|-----------|-----------------|---------|
| 0.8% | 1-12 kb | Large fragments, HMW DNA QC |
| **1.0%** | **0.5-7 kb** | **General purpose, most PCR products** |
| **1.5%** | **0.2-3 kb** | **Genotyping PCR (typical band sizes 500-1100 bp)** |
| 2.0% | 0.1-2 kb | Small fragments, distinguishing bands that are close in size |

**For training and genotyping: use 1.5%.** This is what the [[gel-electrophoresis]] protocol uses.

## Procedure

### 1. Calculate amounts

For a standard mini-gel (~50 mL):

| Agarose % | Agarose (g) | 1x TAE (mL) |
|-----------|-------------|-------------|
| 1.0% | 0.50 g | 50 mL |
| 1.5% | 0.75 g | 50 mL |
| 2.0% | 1.00 g | 50 mL |

For a larger gel (~100 mL), double the amounts.

### 2. Weigh agarose

Weigh the appropriate amount of [[agarose]] on the [[top-loading-balance]]. Transfer to an Erlenmeyer flask.

### 3. Add buffer

Add the appropriate volume of **1x TAE buffer** to the flask.

### 4. Melt

**Microwave method (standard):**

1. Swirl the flask to suspend the agarose.
2. Microwave on high for 30-second intervals, swirling between each interval.
3. **Watch carefully.** The solution will boil and can overflow. Stop and swirl when it starts to bubble vigorously.
4. Continue until the solution is completely clear with no visible particles or streaks. This usually takes 1-2 minutes total.
5. **Caution:** The flask is very hot. Use a heat-resistant glove or towel to handle it.

### 5. Cool

Let the flask cool until you can comfortably hold it (~55-60C). If you pour too hot, the gel tray can warp. If you wait too long, the agarose will start to solidify in the flask.

### 6. Pour

1. Set up the casting tray in its mold with the rubber dams in place (or tape if your tray doesn't have dams).
2. Insert the comb(s) at the appropriate position.
3. Pour the melted agarose slowly and evenly into the tray. Avoid bubbles. If you see bubbles, pop them with a pipette tip before the gel sets.
4. Let the gel solidify at room temperature for 20-30 minutes. It will turn from clear to slightly opaque.

### 7. Remove comb and dams

1. Gently pull the comb straight up out of the gel. This creates the wells where you will load your samples.
2. Remove the rubber dams or tape.
3. The gel is ready to use. Transfer it to the [[gel-electrophoresis-tank|electrophoresis tank]] and cover with 1x TAE buffer.

## Storage

- Cast gels can be stored in 1x TAE buffer at 4C for 1-2 days if needed. Wrap in plastic wrap to prevent drying.
- Do not freeze gels.
