- Cryo gloves (loose-fitting, inspect annually — see [[cryogens-sop]] Administrative Controls §17)
- Safety glasses **or** splash goggles
- **Face shield** for any pressurized transfer
- Closed-toe shoes, long pants (no cuffs — cuffs can catch liquid LN₂ and cause burns)

8. Never cap or seal a small dewar for transport — pressure from evaporation can rupture the container.

## Transport

- **Never in glass.** Use an insulated metal or plastic dewar rated for LN₂.
- **Never in a sealed container.** Evaporation pressure will burst anything airtight.
- **Never in an elevator with yourself.** If the elevator gets stuck, LN₂ can displace enough oxygen to asphyxiate. Put the dewar in the elevator, take the stairs, meet it on the other floor.
- If you *must* share an elevator, travel separately.

## What to do if something goes wrong

- **Spill (small):** evacuate, let the room ventilate, contact EH&S at **(530) 752-1493** for oxygen deficiency monitoring before re-entry.
- **Spill (large) or tank failure:** evacuate the room, pull the fire alarm, follow the building emergency response plan.
- **Skin contact:** treat like a burn. Run cool (not cold) water over the affected area, do not rub, seek medical attention if the skin turns white/waxy or blisters. LN₂ contact can cause frostbite-like injuries in seconds.
- **Any incident:** notify Grey and Kehan. See [[cryogens-sop]] §ADMINISTRATIVE CONTROLS for the reporting chain.
