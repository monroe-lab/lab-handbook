# Lab Orientation: Where Things Are

## Resources

**Location:** Robbins Hall Room 0170 (main lab), Asmussen Hall (growth chambers)

**Related Protocols:** [[bench-cleanup]], [[waste-disposal-quick-reference]]

**Purpose:** Learn the physical layout of the lab before doing any bench work. This is required reading and a walkthrough, not a bench task. After completing this orientation, you should be able to find any piece of equipment, reagent, or waste stream without asking.

## Time estimate

**Wall time:** ~1.5 hr | **Hands-on:** 1.5 hr (guided walkthrough)

---

## Robbins 170 Layout

Robbins 170 is the main lab space. It is organized into bays, each with benches for different types of work. Walk through the entire space with your mentor or Grey before your first day of bench work.

### Bench Areas

The lab has multiple bays with designated benches. As you walk through, note which benches are designated for:

- **General molecular biology** (DNA extraction, PCR setup, gel loading)
- **Sterile work** (the laminar flow clean bench for plating, seed work)
- **Chemistry** (buffer preparation, pH adjustment, weighing)

### Dry Chemical and Reagent Storage

The chemistry area contains:

- **Analytical balance** (the one with glass doors on the sides, for milligram-range measurements)
- **Top-loading balance** (open platform, for gram-range measurements)
- **pH meter**
- **Dry chemical storage** (shelves and cabinets near the balances)

These are all grouped together near each other. Buffer preparation, weighing, and pH adjustment all happen in this area.

### Chemical Cabinets

Chemicals are stored by hazard class, not alphabetically:

- **Flammable cabinet** (yellow, self-closing) — ethanol, methanol, isopropanol, chloroform
- **Corrosive cabinet** — acids (HCl, H2SO4), bases (NaOH, KOH)
- **General chemical cabinet** — non-hazardous reagents, salts, buffers

Know which cabinet a chemical belongs in before you put it back. Check the label or SDS if unsure. See [[how-to-read-an-sds]].

### Cold Storage

- **4C refrigerator** — enzymes, kits, buffers that need cold storage, plates with growing seedlings (stratification)
- **-20C freezer** — primers, competent cells, some enzymes, SYBR Gold aliquots
- **-80C freezer** — tissue samples, long-term DNA storage, flash-frozen material

Every item in the freezer should be logged in the [inventory system](../inventory-app/index.html). When you put something in or take something out, update the inventory.

### Consumables

Know where to find:

- **Pipette tips** (various sizes: P10, P20, P200, P1000; filtered and unfiltered)
- **Microcentrifuge tubes** (1.5 mL, 2 mL)
- **PCR tubes and strip tubes**
- **Falcon tubes** (15 mL, 50 mL)
- **Petri dishes** (for plating)
- **Gloves** (nitrile, correct size for your hands)
- **Parafilm, foil, labeling tape**

If any consumable is running low, note it in the inventory system. You do not order supplies yourself. Mark the item as needs-ordering and notify Grey or your mentor.

## Asmussen Hall — Growth Chambers

Plant growth chambers are in Asmussen Hall, not in Robbins. You will visit the chambers when planting and checking on plants.

## Shared Spaces

## After the Walkthrough

You should be able to answer all of these without looking anything up:

- Where are the pipette tips stored?
- Where is the analytical balance?
- Which cabinet does ethanol go in?
- Where is the -80C freezer?
- Where do sharps go?
- How do you get to the growth chambers?
- Where is the autoclave?

If you can't answer any of these, walk through the lab again.

## Documentation

Create a lab notebook entry for your orientation. Date it. Note anything you want to remember about the layout, any questions that came up, and anything that was unclear. Cite this protocol.
