# Make TAE Buffer (50x Stock)

## Resources

**Equipment:** Top-loading balance, graduated cylinder, stir plate + stir bar, 1 L bottle

**Reagents:** Tris base, glacial acetic acid, EDTA disodium salt (Na2EDTA), distilled water

**Related Protocols:** [[how-to-use-the-scale]], [[gel-electrophoresis]], [[making-an-agarose-gel]]

**Prerequisites:** [[how-to-use-the-scale]], [[ph-measurement]]

**Purpose:** Make a 50x stock of TAE (Tris-Acetate-EDTA) buffer, the running buffer for agarose gel electrophoresis. You will dilute this to 1x when casting and running gels.

## Time estimate

**Wall time:** ~1 hr (mostly dissolving) | **Hands-on:** ~30 min

---

## Before You Start

Check that you have sufficient stock of:

- Tris base (~242 g needed for 1 L of 50x)
- Glacial acetic acid (~57 mL needed)
- EDTA disodium salt (~37 g needed) OR 0.5 M EDTA stock (~100 mL needed)

If anything is low or out, mark it as needs-ordering in the [inventory system](../inventory-app/index.html) before proceeding.

## Background

TAE buffer serves two purposes in gel electrophoresis:

1. **Conducts electricity.** DNA is negatively charged and migrates toward the positive electrode when voltage is applied. The ions in TAE carry the current through the gel.
2. **Maintains pH.** Tris is a buffer that keeps the pH around 8.0-8.5 during electrophoresis, preventing DNA degradation.

The EDTA chelates divalent cations (Mg2+, Ca2+) that nucleases need to function, providing additional protection for your DNA samples.

**50x vs. 1x:** You make a concentrated 50x stock for storage efficiency. Before use, dilute 1 part 50x stock + 49 parts water = 1x working concentration. For a typical gel: 20 mL 50x TAE + 980 mL water = 1 L of 1x TAE.

## Recipe: 1 L of 50x TAE

| Component | Amount | Final concentration (50x) | Final concentration (1x) |
|-----------|--------|--------------------------|-------------------------|
| Tris base | 242 g | 2 M | 40 mM |
| Glacial acetic acid | 57.1 mL | 1 M | 20 mM |
| EDTA disodium salt | 37.2 g | 100 mM | 2 mM |
| Distilled water | to 1 L | | |

**Alternative using 0.5 M EDTA stock:** Replace the 37.2 g EDTA powder with 100 mL of 0.5 M EDTA (pH 8.0). Adjust water accordingly.

## Procedure

1. Weigh **242 g Tris base** on the top-loading balance. Transfer to a 1 L beaker or bottle.
2. Add approximately **800 mL distilled water**. Add a stir bar and place on the stir plate. Stir until dissolved. Tris dissolves slowly; this may take 10-15 minutes.
3. While stirring, carefully add **57.1 mL glacial acetic acid**. Use a graduated cylinder. Glacial acetic acid is corrosive and has a strong odor; pour in the fume hood if available.
4. Add **37.2 g EDTA disodium salt** (or 100 mL of 0.5 M EDTA stock). Stir until dissolved. EDTA dissolves slowly at neutral pH; the acetic acid helps.
5. Bring the total volume to **1 L** with distilled water.
6. The pH of 50x TAE should be approximately **8.0-8.5**. You do not need to adjust pH if you used the correct amounts. If you want to verify, check with the pH meter.
7. Transfer to a labeled 1 L bottle.

## Labeling

Label the bottle with:
- `50x TAE`
- Date prepared
- Your initials

## Storage

- Room temperature. 50x TAE is stable for at least 1 year.
- If you see crystals forming (EDTA can precipitate at low temperature), warm the bottle and swirl to redissolve.

## Making 1x Working Solution

For 1 L of 1x TAE: **20 mL of 50x TAE** + **980 mL distilled water**. Mix well.

You need 1x TAE for both casting the gel and filling the electrophoresis tank. A typical gel run uses 300-500 mL of 1x TAE depending on the tank.
