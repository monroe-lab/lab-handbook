# Sanger on PCR product


## Resources

**Reagents:** [[nuclease-free-water]]

**Consumables:** [[dna-lobind-tubes]], [[pcr-strip-tubes-0-2ml]]

**Related Protocols:** [[pcr-genotyping]], [[gel-electrophoresis]], [[quick-dna-extraction]], [[primer-design]]

### 1. Remove primers from your DNA sample by enzymatic digestion

Mix the following:

- 1 µl of PCR product
- 0.2 µl of EXO-CIP A
- 0.2 µl of EXO-CIP B

Incubate at 37℃ for 4 min (digestion) followed by 80 ℃ 1 min (deactivation)

### 2. Mix with sequencing primer

To the SimpleSeq kit (Satoyo’s bench/left side’s drawer) tube, add the whole amount of your EXO-CIP treated sample, primer (to the final concentration of 2-10 pmol/µl) and volume up to 10 µl. I typically do this process by diluting the EXO-CIP sample with the 5 µM primer stock up to 10 µl.

More info on how to prepare samples for [eurofin submission](https://eurofinsgenomics.com/en/products/dna-sequencing/sample-submission-guidelines/)

# Sanger on Plasmid

### Mix with sequencing primer

To the SimpleSeq kit (Satoyo’s bench/left side’s drawer) tube, add 300 ng ~ 750 ng of plasmid, primer (to the final concentration of 2-10 pmol/µl) and volume up to 10 µl. I typically do this process by putting 500 ng of plasmid into the tube and adding 5 µM primer stock to 10 µl.
