# Pistachio DNA Extraction (Short-Read)

Pre-treatment:

- Perform all centrifugation steps at room temperature (15–25°C).
- Add ethanol to Buffer AW1 and Buffer AW2 as instructed by the manufacturer.
- Redissolve any precipitates in Buffer AP1 and Buffer AW1 concentrates.
- Preheat Buffer EB to 65°C in a heating block or water bath.

Protocol:

1. Weigh 80–100 mg of fresh tissue and put two medium-sized beads into each 2ml microtube, keep the tubes submerged in liquid nitrogen to prevent thawing, then grind the samples into a fine powder using a TissueLyser.
2. Add 800 μL of Buffer AP1 (Lysis Buffer) and 10 μL of RNase A. Vortex vigorously and incubate for 20 minutes at 65°C. Invert the tube every 3–5 minutes during the incubation. (Note: The AP1 volume is adjusted to 800 µL to ensure sufficient lysis without over-diluting the reagents.)
3. Add 260 μL of Buffer P3. Mix thoroughly by inverting the tube, then incubate for 10–15 minutes at room temperature (to precipitate polysaccharides and proteins).
4. Centrifuge the lysate for 5 min at 20,000 x g (14,000 rpm).
5. Pipet the lysate into a QIAshredder spin column placed in a 2 ml collection tube (650 μL per spin). Centrifuge for 2 min at 20,000 x g. Repeat if there is remaining lysate.
6. Carefully transfer the flow-through into a new tube without disturbing the pellet. Estimate or measure the exact volume of the flow-through.
7. Add exactly 1.5 volumes of Buffer AW1 to the flow-through (Ideally you will add Buffer AW1 to the 2 mL line), and mix thoroughly by pipetting.
8. Transfer 650 μl of the mixture into a DNeasy Mini spin column. Centrifuge for 1 min at ≥6000 x g (≥8000 rpm). Discard the flow-through. Repeat this step until all mixture is processed.
9. Place the spin column into a new 2 mL collection tube. Add 500 μL of Buffer AW2, close the cap securely, and invert the tube to wash the inner walls. Centrifuge for 1 min at ≥6,000 x g, then discard the flow-through.
10. Add another 500 μl Buffer AW2, invert the tube again. Centrifuge for 2 min at 20,000 x g.
11. Add 500 μl of freshly prepared 70% EtOH, centrifuge for 1 min at ≥6,000 x g, then discard the flow-through.
12. Add another 500 μl of 70% EtOH. Centrifuge for 2 min at 20,000 x g.
13. Discard the flow-through. Centrifuge the EMPTY column again for 2 min at 20,000 x g to completely dry the membrane. Then, open the cap and let it sit at a heat block (65°C)for 3-5 minutes to allow any residual ethanol to evaporate.
14. Transfer the spin column to a new 1.5 ml microcentrifuge tube.
15. Elution: Add 35 μl of PRE-WARMED (55°C) Buffer EB directly to the center of the membrane. Incubate for 5 min at Room Temperature or 55°C. Centrifuge for 1 min at ≥6000 x g to elute. (For maximum yield, you can take the eluted flow-through and re-apply it to the column, incubate for 2 mins, and spin again).

1. Elution (Step 12)

- Adding Buffer EB and incubating at 4°C for one hour.
- Recommendation: Pre-warm Buffer EB to 65°C. Apply it to the column and incubate at room temperature or 65°C for 5 minutes. To maximize concentration, you can perform a two-step elution (e.g., apply 25 µL of pre-warmed AE, incubate and spin; then reload the flow-through back onto the membrane or use a fresh 25 µL of AE for a second spin).

2. Starting Material Overload (Step 1)

- Using ~100 mg of tissue.
- Recommendation: 80~100mg, I like to weigh out 85mg.

3. Dealing with Polyphenols and Polysaccharides (Steps 2 & 3)

- Using only Buffer AP1 and P3.
- Recommendation 1 (Lysis Phase): Before adding AP1, supplement it with 1%–2% PVP (Polyvinylpyrrolidone, MW 40,000) or 0.2% β-mercaptoethanol. PVP specifically binds to polyphenols, acting like a sponge, while β-mercaptoethanol provides a reducing environment to prevent polyphenol oxidation.
- Recommendation 2 (Precipitation Phase): After adding Buffer P3, extend the ice incubation time from 5 minutes to 10–15 minutes. A longer ice bath allows for much more thorough precipitation of proteins, polysaccharides, and SDS complexes, significantly improving the clarity of the lysate before column loading.

4. Imprecise Binding Step (Step 6)

- Current practice: "Add Buffer AW1 to the 2 mL line (~1000 – 1100 µL)."
- Buffer AW1 (which contains ethanol) is used to adjust the high-salt and ethanol concentrations required for DNA to bind to the silica membrane. Visually estimating the volume leads to inaccurate ethanol concentrations. Too low, and the DNA fails to bind; too high, and impurities will co-precipitate.
- Recommendation: Use precise pipetting. Carefully measure the exact volume of the recovered supernatant (let's call it V µL), and then add exactly 1.5 volumes (1.5 × V µL) of Buffer AW1.

5. Ethanol Carryover Issue (Step 10)

- Letting the column sit at room temperature for 1 minute for the wash buffer to drip down.
- Buffer AW2 contains a high concentration of ethanol. Even trace amounts of ethanol carryover will severely ruin your A260/280 and A260/230 ratios (resulting in low purity readings) and will inhibit downstream PCR.
- Recommendation: After discarding the flow-through from the final AW2 wash, place the empty column back into the collection tube and perform a "dry spin" at maximum speed (20,000 x g) for 2–3 minutes. Then, open the cap and let the column air-dry at room temperature for 2–5 minutes to ensure all residual ethanol has completely evaporated.
