In general, we don't do  High Throughput, but the following method works for all plant samples. We use the [https://journals.plos.org/plosone/article?id=10.1371/journal.pone.0206085](https://journals.plos.org/plosone/article?id=10.1371/journal.pone.0206085) (sorbitol pre-wash + high-salt CTAB)

with following modifications;

(i) we used [[sodium-metabisulfite]] (1% w/v) instead of [[2-mercaptoethanol]] (1% v/v) in the sorbitol wash buffer and CTAB solution; 

(ii) we repeated the tissue homogenate wash steps until the supernatant turned clear;

(iii) we performed the CTAB lysis step at 45°C and

(iv) the [[chloroform]] extraction step twice using ice-cold chloroform.

Inglis et al. PLOS 2018 Paper

- Repeat sorbitol wash steps until supernatant is CLEAR

- Repeat chloroform extraction step twice.

- Other modifications to test:

- Add BOTH 1% (w/v or v/v) sodium metabisulfite and BME in the CTAB buffer
- Even increasing BME concentration to 2% (v/v)

- For HMW DNA extraction:

- Remove beads after tissues are initially ground (before sorbitol wash is added)

PC extraction w/ Alcohol Precipitation

[https://onlinelibrary.wiley.com/doi/10.1111/1755-0998.13588](https://onlinelibrary.wiley.com/doi/10.1111/1755-0998.13588)

2.7 Phenol/chloroform extraction (PC)

We extracted DNA using a phenol/chloroform extraction protocol modified from Chapter 6, protocol 1 of Sambrook et al. (1989). For SDS cell lysis, we added 500 µl of pooled sample to 10 ml of TLB (10 mm Tris-HCL pH 8.0, 25 mm EDTA pH 8.0, 100 mm NaCl, 0.5% [w/v] SDS, 20 µg ml–1 RNase A) and vortexed at full speed for 5 s followed by an incubation at 37°C for 1 h. Proteinase K (Qiagen) was added to a final concentration of 200 µg ml–1 and we mixed the sample by slow inversion three times, followed by 2 h at 50°C with gentle mixing every 30 min.

We purified the lysate with 10 ml buffer saturated phenol using phase-lock gel falcon tubes, followed by phenol/chloroform-isoamyl alcohol (1:1).

We precipitated the DNA by adding 4 ml of 5 m [[ammonium-acetate]] and 30 ml ice-cold ethanol.

We recovered DNA by one of the following methods:

1. a glass hook followed by washing twice in 70% ethanol (if a DNA mass was visible) or
2. centrifugation at 4500 g for 10 min followed by washing twice in 70% ethanol (if no DNA mass was visible).
3. After spinning down at 10,000 x g, we removed ethanol by drying at ambient temperature for 10 min. We added 100 µl EB (elution buffer, 10 mm Tris-HCl, pH 8.5) to the DNA and left it at 4°C overnight to resuspend the pellet.

Working Protocol

HiFi Extraction for Challenging Plant Samples

Based on [Inglis et al. 2018](https://journals.plos.org/plosone/article?id=10.1371/journal.pone.0206085), with modifications for HMW DNA. The final DNA cleanup step is based on the DIY HiFi extraction for Arabidopsis (based on modified protocols by [Rabanal et al. 2022](https://paperpile.com/c/Ov8wZy/6421), [Russo et al. 2022](https://paperpile.com/c/Ov8wZy/JoWJ) and [Mayjonade et al. 2016](https://paperpile.com/c/Ov8wZy/veB8)).

The protocol takes approximately 5-6 hours to complete.

List of other consumables, enzymes, etc.

- PureLinkTM RNAseA (Thermo Fisher Scientific; #12091021), or 100 mg/ml DNase-free RNase A /(Qiagen, Germantown, MD, United  States)
- 24:1 (v/v) chloroform:isoamyl alcohol
- Qiagen EB Buffer or 10 mM Tris-HCl pH 8.5
- 0.4% solution of SeraMag SpeedBeads® Carboxyl Magnetic Beads (#65152105050250, Fisher  Scientific) prepared  as  in Schalamun et al. (2019)
- Collibri DNA beads kit
- 80% ethanol (freshly prepared)
- Wide-bore pipet tips (P1000 and P200)
- 2ml tubes
- DNA lo-bind 1.5mL tubes
- Magnetic Separation Rack

- Measure out ~150mg of fresh tissue per sample and add 2 x 6.3mm beads into tube
- Place weighed samples into LN / -80 ̊C until use
- To scale up to 15ml reactions, measure 1 - 1.5 g of tissue and grind in LN with mortar and pestle.

1. Add an equal volume (as aqueous phase after CI) of 0.4% SeraMag beads and mix by pipetting with a wide-bore tip. Incubate for 30 min at 10rpm at RT on the hula mixer.
2. Spin tubes down and place on a magnetic rack. Wait until the solution becomes clear. Remove supernatant.
3. Add 1ml of 80% EtOH and immediately resuspend the bead pellet by inverting 15-20 times. Place the tube back on the magnetic rack, wait for the solution to become clear. Remove the supernatant.

- Make sure to work quickly after ethanol is added. Too much time in ethanol will cause beads to clump together making it difficult to elute later on.

4. Repeat step 3 (EtOH wash) again.
5. Add 50ul of pre-heated (45-50C) EB buffer, and incubate for 15min x 37̊C x 10rpm.

- The hulamixer fits into the 37̊C incubator.

6. Spin tubes down and place on a magnetic rack. Wait for the solution to become clear. Transfer the supernatant into a new 1.5 ml tube using a wide-bore tip.

- Make sure to use a low binding tube.

7. Add 20ul of Collibri beads and mix with a wide-bore tip. Incubate for 15min x 37̊C x 10rpm. Repeat steps 2-6.
8. Leave the tube on your bench overnight.

- To allow HMW DNA to homogenize in solution

9. Measure the concentration on Qubit BR and [[nanodrop]].

Pre-Wash

Remove polyphenols and polysaccharides from tissue macerates prior to cell lysis

Lysis Buffer

CTAB:

- Cationic detergent; in aqueous solution, captures the membrane lipids to form a hybrid micelle
- Function differs based on ionic strength of the solution:

- at LOW ionic strengths, precipitates nucleic acids & acidic polysaccharides while protein & neutral polysaccharides remain in solution
- at HIGH ionic concentration, binds to polysaccharides to form complexes that are removed during subsequent chloroform extraction

- Denatures/inhibits activity of proteins/enzymes (e.g DNases, RNases)
- Keep cell lysis solution/buffer with CTAB in a water bath at 60-65C, which irreversibly inhibits DNase.
- Cool at RT before CI/PCI/CO is added.

NaCl:

- Remove proteins bound to DNA & helps to keep them dissolved in aqueous layer so they don’t precipitate in alcohol along with DNA by neutralizing -ve charges on DNA
- Osmotic pressure / cell lysis
- Salt [ ] > 0.5M provides ionic strength needed for CTAB to precipitate polysaccharides

- 1.4M suggested in many protocols; higher NaCl and/or CTAB recommended to get rid of samples rich in polysaccharides

EDTA

- Chelates divalent cations (Mg2+, Ca2+, etc.) which are important cofactors for RNase and DNase (enzymes)

PVP

- Polymer; to remove polyphenolic contamination by binding them through H-bonds
- Prevents polyphenol oxidation
- During chloroform centrifugation, PVP complexes accumulate b/w the phases

Sodium Metabisulfite (Na2S2O5) - [source](https://www.tandfonline.com/doi/full/10.2144/btn-2021-0050%23:~:text=The%2520addition%2520of%2520sodium%2520metabisulfite,compounds%2520on%2520the%2520extracted%2520DNA.)

- Reducing agent to prevent oxidation of phenolics / effects of reactive phenolic compounds on extracted DNA

B-mercaptoethanol

- Reducing agent; prevents oxidation of proteins, tannins, phenolics
- Can denature proteins by breaking intermolecular disulfide linkages  (to make them insoluble in water)

Why use sodium metabisulfite OR BME rather than BOTH?

PCI / CI - DNA Extraction

Chloroform

- Nonpolar solvent in which nonpolar proteins & lipids become dissolved in and isolating DNA in the aqueous phase (density 1.47g/cm^3)

Isoamyl Alcohol

- To prevent emulsification of the solution & keep phases separated
- Helps to inhibit RNase activity & preventing solubilization in the phenol phase long RNA molecules along with long poly(A) portions

Alcohol Precipitation - Salting Out

Isopropanol/Ethanol

- To precipitate DNA out of extraction solution & wash away salts and other reagents
- Breaks hydration shell around DNA by forming H-bonds (DNA remains dissolved in aqueous solution because of its phosphodiester backbone which is hydrophilic)
- Also dissolves nonpolar solvents (e.g. chloroform) to further remove impurities
- Use RT isopropanol (as cold isopropanol may also precipitate polysaccharides)

Choice between Isopropanol and Ethanol?

- Volume thing…..

Sodium Acetate / Ammonium Acetate/ [[potassium-acetate]]:

- In solution, sodium acetate dissociates into Na+ and [CH3COO]−

- Na+ neutralize negative charges on sugar phosphate backbone of DNA (PO3- groups) reducing repulsion b/w DNA molecules → makes DNA far less hydrophilic & thus less soluble in water

- With ethanol/isopropanol which makes the solution have a low dielectric constant, allows Na+ to interact with PO3- allowing the “salting out” of DNA from the solution
