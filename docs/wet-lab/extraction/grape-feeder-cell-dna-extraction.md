# Grape Feeder Cell DNA Extraction

**Time estimate:** ~2.5-3 hr hands-on, depending on how many charcoal washes the cells need. Mostly waiting on sedimentation steps + DNeasy column spins.

> **Disclaimer:** This protocol was generated by Claude (Anthropic) based on a conversation with Grey on 2026-05-02. Verify everything independently before doing anything in the lab. This is a starting point, not a validated SOP.

## Resources

**Equipment:** [[centrifuge|Microcentrifuge]], [[water-bath]] or [[heat-block]] (65°C), [[vortex-mixer]], inverted light microscope (in tissue culture facility, e.g. Nikon Eclipse Ti or Zeiss Axio Vert)

**Reagents:** [[dneasy-plant-mini|QIAGEN DNeasy Plant Mini Kit]], [[rnase-aliquot|RNase A]], 0.4 M mannitol (wash buffer), [[ethanol-absolute|100% ethanol]] (for DNeasy buffer prep)

**Optional:** acid-washed sand or zirconia beads (mechanical disruption if needed), pectolyase Y-23 (loosen embedded charcoal — only if differential sedimentation fails), 40-70 µm cell strainer (only if filtering is the path forward), NaCl 5 M stock (high-salt wash variant)

**Related Protocols:** [[qiagen-dneasy-extraction]], [[quantifying-dna-qubit]], [[gel-electrophoresis]], [[pcr-genotyping]]

**Contacts:** [[percival-singson]], [[grey-monroe]]

**Project:** [[grape-protoplast-ltee]]

**Purpose:** Extract sequencing-quality genomic DNA from grape embryogenic feeder cell suspension cultures (1103P rootstock) grown in charcoal-containing medium. The challenge is the activated charcoal — if present at lysis, it adsorbs the DNA. Target yield: ≥500 ng per sample for Illumina DNA Prep input.

## Before You Start

Check that you have sufficient stock of:

- [[dneasy-plant-mini|DNeasy Plant Mini Kit]] (one column per sample)
- [[rnase-aliquot|RNase A]]
- [[ethanol-absolute|100% ethanol]] (for kit buffer prep on first use)
- 0.4 M mannitol — make fresh as wash buffer, 50 mL per ~6 samples

If anything is low, mark needs-ordering in the inventory system.

**First time using the kit:** Add ethanol to AW1 + AW2 per kit handbook. Done once, lasts the kit.

## Background

### Why charcoal is the problem

Activated charcoal binds nucleic acids non-specifically — hydrophobic + electrostatic interactions. The medium contains 2 g/L of it, and feeder cells live their whole life in a charcoal slurry. **Charcoal particles are physically stuck to cell surfaces.** Lysing cells while charcoal is present means most of the DNA gets adsorbed and lost.

You can't just pour off the medium. The wash matters.

### The strategy

1. **Separate cells from charcoal** by differential sedimentation (charcoal denser → settles faster than cells).
2. **Confirm separation** under a light microscope before lysis.
3. **Lyse the washed cells** with DNeasy. Cell walls are thin and soft (callus is much weaker than leaf), so vortex + heat alone may suffice; mechanical disruption only if yield's poor.
4. **QC** — concentration (Qubit), purity (NanoDrop ratios), integrity (gel), enzymatic competence (PCR).

## Step 1 — Separate cells from charcoal

This is the hardest part and where you'll iterate.

### Differential sedimentation (try first)

| ◻︎ | 1. | Transfer feeder suspension to a 15 or 50 mL conical tube. |
| --- | --- | --- |
| ◻︎ | 2. | Let stand **30-60 sec**. Charcoal settles first; cells stay turbid in supernatant. |
| ◻︎ | 3. | Carefully aspirate the cell-containing turbid supernatant into a fresh tube. Leave the dense charcoal-rich bottom layer behind. |
| ◻︎ | 4. | Repeat 2-3 wash cycles with **0.4 M mannitol** (resuspend gently, let stand, transfer top layer). |
| ◻︎ | 5. | Final spin: 100-200 × g, 2 min, RT. Discard supernatant, keep cell pellet. |

### If charcoal won't separate cleanly

- **High-salt wash variant.** Add 100-200 mM NaCl to mannitol wash. Disrupts electrostatic charcoal-cell adhesion. Try this before more invasive options.
- **Cell strainer (40-70 µm).** Filter the suspension through a strainer. Works if charcoal particles and cells are different enough in size. Test empirically.
- **Brief gentle pectolyase Y-23 treatment.** 0.05-0.1% pectolyase, 5-10 min at RT, in 0.4 M mannitol. Loosens charcoal embedded in extracellular matrix without fully digesting the cell wall. Last resort — adds complexity.

### Microscope check (mandatory)

| ◻︎ | 6. | Take a small aliquot of washed cell pellet, resuspend in 0.4 M mannitol. |
| ◻︎ | 7. | Pipette ~10 µL onto a slide, view at **4× or 10×** brightfield on inverted microscope. |
| ◻︎ | 8. | Confirm: callus cells visible (20-100 µm), no opaque black charcoal granules. |

If you still see charcoal particles, redo the wash. Don't proceed to lysis with visible charcoal.

## Step 2 — DNeasy lysis

| ◻︎ | 1. | Resuspend washed cell pellet in 400 µL Buffer AP1 + 4 µL RNase A. |
| ◻︎ | 2. | Vortex vigorously 30 sec. |
| ◻︎ | 3. | Incubate 10 min at 65°C. Invert tube 2-3× during incubation. |
| ◻︎ | 4. | If lysate looks chunky or yields are poor on prior runs: add a pinch of acid-washed sand or 2-3 small zirconia beads, vortex 30 sec to mechanically disrupt walls. |

## Step 3 — Standard DNeasy

From here, follow [[qiagen-dneasy-extraction]] from the P3 step onward:

- 130 µL Buffer P3, mix, ice 5 min
- Spin into QIAshredder column, 2 min, max speed
- Transfer flowthrough, add 1.5× volume Buffer AW1, mix
- Bind to DNeasy spin column, wash with AW1 then AW2 (2× recommended)
- Dry spin
- Elute in **50-100 µL** pre-warmed (55°C) Buffer AE / EB. 5 min RT incubation before final spin.

Lower elution volume → higher concentration. 50 µL gives best Qubit ng/µL for downstream library prep input. 100 µL preserves total yield.

## Step 4 — QC

### Concentration

- **NanoDrop** for quick read + 260/280 + 260/230. Tends to overestimate.
- **Qubit BR** for accurate dsDNA quantification. **This is the number that matters for library prep.**

### Purity

- 260/280: target ≥1.8. <1.8 → protein contamination.
- 260/230: target ≥1.8. <1.8 → polysaccharide / phenol / salt carryover. Common with plant material; can inhibit library prep enzymes.

### Integrity

- Run on 0.8% agarose, 1× TAE, 80V, 30-45 min.
- Want a tight high-MW band, minimal smearing. Smear → degraded DNA.

### Enzymatic competence (PCR validation)

Before sending anything for sequencing:

- Amplify a known grape housekeeping gene — *VvACTIN1* or *VvGAPDH*. Stock primers may already exist in [[primer-stocks-grape]] (check inventory) or order from IDT.
- Clean band on a gel = DNA is enzymatically competent. You're good to proceed to library prep.

## Targets

| Metric | Target |
|---|---|
| Total yield | **≥500 ng per sample** (10 ng/µL × 50 µL elution) |
| 260/280 | ≥1.8 |
| 260/230 | ≥1.8 |
| Gel | tight HMW band, minimal smear |
| PCR | clean single band on housekeeping gene |

If a sample falls short on yield, **pool 2 extractions from the same time point** rather than push library prep at low input.

## Troubleshooting

| Symptom | Likely cause | Fix |
|---|---|---|
| Yield <100 ng | Charcoal still present at lysis | Redo wash, microscope-confirm before lysis |
| Yield 100-400 ng | Cell walls under-disrupted | Add bead/sand mechanical step |
| Bad 260/280 (<1.7) | Protein carryover | Add second AW2 wash, longer dry spin |
| Bad 260/230 (<1.7) | Polysaccharides / ethanol carryover | Longer dry spin, 65°C heat block 3-5 min before elution. Pre-bind 0.5× SPRI cleanup as last resort. |
| PCR fails despite OK Qubit | RNA inflating Qubit, real DNA is low; or PCR inhibitor | Confirm with Qubit BR (dsDNA-specific). Add inhibitor-tolerant polymerase or dilute template. |
| Cells lyse during charcoal wash | Mannitol osmotic balance off | Confirm 0.4 M concentration; cells should be osmotically stable here |

## Notes for first-time runs

- Charcoal-cell separation will take iteration. Plan a half-day for the first sample, not a 30-minute wash.
- Document every variation in lab notebook: how many washes, which buffer, sedimentation time, microscope observations, yield, ratios. Future-Percy + future-cohort needs this.
- Take a photo of the microscope view before and after final wash. Saves answering "did the wash work" by memory.
