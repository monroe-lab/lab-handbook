# Seed Sterilization

**Purpose:** Surface-sterilize Arabidopsis seeds before plating on sterile MS media.

**Source:** Satoyo Oya's protocols

---

## Background

Arabidopsis seeds carry fungal spores and bacteria on their seed coat from the environment they were harvested in. If you plate unsterilized seeds on nutrient-rich MS media, you'll get fungal lawns within days that kill the seedlings. The goal of sterilization is to kill surface microbes without damaging the embryo inside.

**Why these chemicals work:**

| Agent | Mechanism | Pros | Cons |
|-------|-----------|------|------|
| **70% ethanol** | Disrupts cell membranes, denatures proteins. 70% is more effective than 100% because water helps ethanol penetrate cells. | Fast, simple, no rinse needed (evaporates). | Less effective against fungal spores than bleach. |
| **Sodium hypochlorite (bleach)** | Strong oxidizer, destroys proteins and nucleic acids on contact. The active ingredient (NaOCl) breaks down organic matter. | Very effective against fungi and bacteria. | Must rinse thoroughly — residual bleach inhibits germination. |
| **Triton X-100** | Nonionic surfactant (detergent). Reduces surface tension so the sterilization solution wets the seed coat and gets into crevices. | Dramatically improves bleach penetration. | None at these concentrations. |
| **Chlorine gas** | Same chemistry as bleach but in gas phase — penetrates everywhere without liquid handling. | Can sterilize hundreds of tubes at once without opening them. Seeds stay dry. | Toxic gas, must use fume hood. Longer exposure time. |

**Which method to choose:** For this curriculum (small batches of 4 genotypes, <50 seeds each), ethanol is simplest. Bleach/Triton is more thorough and is what the lab uses for high-throughput work. Chlorine gas is for when you're sterilizing seed stocks in bulk.

## Time estimate

| Method | Wall time | Hands-on |
|--------|-----------|----------|
| Ethanol | 20 min | 5 min |
| Bleach/Triton | 40 min | 15 min |
| Chlorine gas | 3-16 hr | 10 min |

---

## Method 1: Ethanol (small batches, <200 seeds)

1. Place seeds in a 1.5 mL tube
2. Add 1 mL **70% ethanol**
3. Place on tube rotator for **10-15 min**
4. Remove ethanol
5. Allow seeds to dry in [[clean-bench|clean bench]] (EtOH evaporates quickly)
6. Proceed to [[wet-lab/stratification]] or plating

> *If you get contamination with this method:* Switch to Method 2. Some seed batches (especially old ones or those harvested in humid conditions) carry spores that ethanol alone won't kill.

---

## Method 2: Bleach/Triton (larger batches, >200 seeds)

### Sterilization solution (make fresh each time — hypochlorite degrades)
| Component | Amount |
|-----------|--------|
| Sodium hypochlorite (household bleach, ~5-8% NaOCl) | 1 mL |
| 20% Triton X-100 | 50 uL |
| Sterile dH2O | to 10 mL |

Final concentration is ~0.5-0.8% NaOCl — enough to kill microbes but mild enough not to damage seeds with short exposure.

### Procedure
1. Place seeds in a 1.5 mL tube
2. Add sterilization solution
3. **First wash:** rotate **5 min** (loosens debris, initial kill)
4. Remove solution, add fresh sterilization solution
5. **Second wash:** rotate **15 min** (thorough sterilization)
6. Remove solution
7. **Rinse 3x** with sterile water (in [[clean-bench|clean bench]]) — this is critical, residual bleach inhibits germination
8. Resuspend in 1 mL sterile water
9. Proceed to [[wet-lab/stratification]] (wrap in foil, 4 C, 2 days)

> *Why two washes?* The first wash loosens organic debris (soil particles, dead tissue) that shields microbes. The second wash hits the now-exposed spores directly.

> *Seeds turning white/translucent:* Over-sterilized. Reduce the second wash to 10 min, or dilute the bleach further. The embryo is damaged if the seed coat is visibly degraded.

---

## Method 3: Chlorine Gas (bulk sterilization, dry seeds)

For sterilizing many tubes simultaneously without opening them. The gas penetrates through the tube opening and sterilizes everything it contacts.

1. Place open tubes of seeds in a desiccator
2. Place a beaker with **100 mL household bleach** inside
3. In the fume hood, add **3 mL concentrated HCl** to the bleach
4. Quickly seal the desiccator (chlorine gas is generated immediately)
5. Leave sealed for **3-16 hours** (longer = more thorough)
6. Open desiccator **in the fume hood** and let gas dissipate
7. Close tubes — seeds are sterilized and dry, ready for plating or storage

**Chemistry:** NaOCl + 2 HCl → Cl2 + NaCl + H2O. The Cl2 gas is the sterilizing agent.

**Safety:** Chlorine gas is toxic — causes severe respiratory damage. Always work in a fume hood. Do not breathe the gas. Do not open the desiccator outside the fume hood.
