# Transferring Seedlings to Soil

## Resources

**Equipment:** Forceps (fine-tipped), pots or cell trays, humidity dome, spray bottle

**Reagents:** Pre-moistened potting soil

**Related Protocols:** [[planting-arabidopsis-on-ms-plates]], [[planting-in-soil]], [[watering-and-plant-care]], [[growth-chamber-operation]]

**Prerequisites:** Plate-grown seedlings with true leaves (~14 days old)

**Purpose:** Transplant seedlings from MS plates to soil for continued growth to maturity. Used when seedlings were started on plates (e.g., after seed sterilization and germination screening) and need to be moved to soil for bulking seed or tissue collection.

## Time estimate

**Wall time:** ~30 min | **Hands-on:** 30 min (for ~20 seedlings)

---

## Before You Start

- Seedlings should have at least 2 true leaves (not just cotyledons). This is typically 10-14 days after planting on plates.
- Prepare pots with **moist potting soil** before you start. The soil should be damp but not waterlogged. Poke a small hole in the center of each pot with a pencil or forceps tip.
- Have a spray bottle with water ready.

## Procedure

1. Open the plate in the clean bench or on a clean bench surface. Plates do not need to remain sterile after this point.
2. Using fine-tipped forceps, gently grasp the seedling at the base of the hypocotyl (the stem between the roots and cotyledons). **Do not squeeze the leaves or the root tip.**
3. Carefully lift the seedling out of the agar. Some agar will cling to the roots; this is fine. Do not try to remove it.
4. Place the seedling into the pre-made hole in the soil. The root should point downward. The cotyledons should be at or just above the soil surface.
5. Gently press the soil around the base of the seedling to hold it in place. Do not compact the soil.
6. Mist the seedling with the spray bottle immediately after transplanting.
7. Repeat for all seedlings.

## After Transplanting

1. Cover the tray with a **humidity dome** (clear plastic lid) for the first 2-3 days. Seedlings transplanted from plates are accustomed to the high humidity of a sealed plate; the dome prevents desiccation shock.
2. After 2-3 days, crack the dome slightly to begin acclimating seedlings to ambient humidity.
3. After 4-5 days, remove the dome entirely.
4. Move the tray to the growth chamber. See [[growth-chamber-operation]].
5. Begin the watering schedule in [[watering-and-plant-care]].

## Label Everything

Label each pot with:
- Genotype / line name
- Date transplanted
- Your initials
- Number of seedlings in the pot (if more than one)

See [[tube-and-sample-labeling]] for conventions.

## Common Problems

| Problem | Cause | Fix |
|---------|-------|-----|
| Seedlings wilt immediately | Humidity shock | Use the dome. Mist frequently for the first 48 hours |
| Seedlings fall over | Not seated deeply enough in soil | Gently re-seat and press soil around base |
| Roots damaged during transfer | Forceps grip too tight or too high | Grip at the hypocotyl base, not the roots |
| Fungal growth on soil surface | Agar carried over from plate, high humidity | Reduce dome humidity earlier. The agar will decompose; fungus is usually not harmful to the seedling |
