# What to Do When Something Breaks

## Resources

**Supplies:** Dustpan and brush, broken glass box, first aid kit, [[nitrile-gloves]]

**Related Protocols:** [[lab-orientation]], [[spill-response]], [[sharps-handling]]

**Purpose:** Immediate action guide for broken glass and equipment failure. Things break in labs. Know what to do.

## Time estimate

**Reading time:** ~10 min

---

## Broken Glassware

### If you are cut

1. Apply pressure with a clean gauze or paper towel.
2. Rinse the wound under running water.
3. Apply a bandage. If the cut is deep, won't stop bleeding, or has glass embedded in it, seek medical attention (UC Davis Student Health: 530-752-2349, or call 911 for serious injuries).
4. Report the injury to Grey.

### Cleanup

1. **Do not pick up broken glass with your bare hands.** Ever.
2. Put on thick gloves (or double up nitrile gloves).
3. Use a dustpan and brush to sweep up large pieces.
4. Use damp paper towels to pick up small fragments and slivers. Press the damp towel onto the area and it will pick up tiny shards.
5. Dispose of all glass (including the paper towels with fragments) in the **broken glass box**, not the regular trash. See [[sharps-handling]].
6. If the broken glass contained chemicals, treat it as a chemical spill first (see [[spill-response]]), then clean up the glass.

## Equipment Failure

### Something isn't working

1. Check the obvious: Is it plugged in? Is it turned on? Is there an error message on the display?
2. Check if someone else is already aware of the problem (look for notes taped to the equipment).
3. If you can't fix it in 2 minutes of troubleshooting, **do not force it**. Report to Grey.
4. If the equipment is shared (autoclave, centrifuge, growth chambers), put a note on it: "Not working as of [date]. Contact [your name]."

### Something is making a bad noise

- **[[centrifuge]] vibrating/grinding:** Press stop immediately. This is an unbalanced rotor. See [[centrifuge-operation]].
- **[[autoclave-sterilizer|Autoclave]] hissing when it shouldn't be:** Do not touch. Leave the area. Notify Grey.
- **Any equipment smoking or sparking:** Turn off at the power strip or wall outlet if you can safely reach it. Leave the area. Call facilities or Grey.

## Report Everything

Every breakage or equipment failure should be reported to Grey, even if you cleaned it up or it seems minor. This is important for:

- Tracking equipment that needs repair or replacement
- Safety records
- Reordering broken supplies

## Documentation

Note any incidents in your lab notebook: date, what broke, how it happened, how you handled it, who you notified.
