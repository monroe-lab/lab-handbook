# Planting Arabidopsis in Soil

**Purpose:** Direct sowing in soil for growing plants to maturity (flowering, crossing, seed set).

---

## Materials

- Pre-mixed potting soil (Arabidopsis-appropriate — fine-grained, well-draining)
- Small pots or flats (individual cells preferred for tracking genotypes)
- Plastic dome / humidity cover
- Labels and markers
- Spray bottle with water
- Seeds (stratified — see [[stratification]])

## Procedure

1. Fill pots/cells with pre-moistened soil (water soil before sowing, not after)
2. Gently press soil surface flat with the bottom of another pot
3. Place 3-5 seeds per pot on the soil surface — **do not bury** (Arabidopsis seeds need light to germinate)
4. Mist lightly with spray bottle
5. Cover with humidity dome
6. Place in **4 C** for 2 days (stratification — can skip if seeds were already stratified in tubes)
7. Move to growth chamber or [[controlled-environment-facility|CEF]]
   - Long day: 16h light / 8h dark
   - 22 C
8. Remove humidity dome after **3-5 days** (once cotyledons are up)
9. Thin to **1 seedling per pot** once true leaves appear (~7-10 days)

## Growth to flowering

- **Col-0:** bolts at ~3-4 weeks, flowers ~4-5 weeks (at 22 C, long day)
- **Kar-1:** expected similar timing (FT16 = 39 days)
- Plants are ready for crossing when first flowers open

## Notes

- For this curriculum, plant both on plates (see [[planting-arabidopsis-on-ms-plates]]) AND in soil
- Soil plants are what you'll actually cross — plate-grown seedlings can be transplanted to soil later, but direct sowing is simpler for the crossing timeline
- Label everything: genotype, date, pot number
- Water from the bottom (sub-irrigation) once plants are established — prevents algae on soil surface

## Location

- Growth chamber space needed — check [[controlled-environment-facility|CEF]] availability or use lab growth chambers
- CEF pesticide schedule: Wednesdays 3-5 PM, doors lock at 2:30 PM
