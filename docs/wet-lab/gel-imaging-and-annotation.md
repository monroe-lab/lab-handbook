# Gel Imaging and Annotation

## Resources

**Equipment:** UV transilluminator (with viewing window), cell phone camera

**Related Protocols:** [[gel-electrophoresis]], [[pcr-genotyping]]

**Prerequisites:** A stained agarose gel (see [[gel-electrophoresis]])

**Purpose:** Photograph your gel, annotate every lane, and save a properly labeled image in your lab notebook. This is the final step of every gel run. An unannotated gel image is almost useless two weeks later.

## Time estimate

**Wall time:** ~10 min | **Hands-on:** ~10 min

---

## Procedure

### 1. Image the gel

1. Place the stained gel on the **UV transilluminator**.
2. Turn on the UV light. DNA bands stained with SYBR Gold will fluoresce bright green/yellow.
3. Look through the **UV-protective viewing window**. **Never look directly at UV light without protection.** The viewing window filters UV.
4. Take a photo through the viewing window with your **cell phone camera**.
5. Take multiple photos if needed. Adjust exposure so bands are clearly visible but not overexposed (washed out).
6. Turn off the UV light when done.

### Tips for good gel photos

- **Steady the phone.** Blurry gel images are worthless. Brace your hands or use a phone stand.
- **Fill the frame.** Get close enough that the gel fills most of the photo. You want to see individual lanes clearly.
- **Include the ladder.** Make sure the DNA ladder lane is visible and its bands are distinguishable.
- **Avoid reflections.** Angle the phone to minimize glare from the viewing window.

### 2. Annotate the image

This is the critical step. An unlabeled gel photo tells you nothing in two weeks.

Open the photo on your phone or computer and annotate it. You can use any image editing tool (phone markup, Preview on Mac, any annotation app). Mark:

1. **Every lane.** Label each lane with the sample identity (e.g., "Col-0", "SALK_045678 #1", "NTC" for no-template control, "Ladder").
2. **Key ladder sizes.** Mark the sizes of the relevant ladder bands (e.g., 1 kb, 500 bp, 250 bp) so you can estimate sample band sizes.
3. **Expected band sizes.** For genotyping gels: note the expected WT band size and T-DNA insertion band size next to the ladder reference.
4. **Visible bands.** Circle or arrow any bands of interest. Note their approximate size.
5. **The genotype call** (for genotyping gels): WT, HET, or HOM for each sample lane.

### 3. Save to lab notebook

Save the annotated image in your **digital lab notebook entry** for this experiment.

The lab notebook entry for a gel run should include:

- Date
- Protocol cited ([[gel-electrophoresis]], [[pcr-genotyping]])
- Gel percentage and buffer
- Sample loading order (which sample in which lane)
- Annotated gel image
- Interpretation (what the bands tell you)

## Example Annotation

For a T-DNA genotyping gel (3-primer strategy):

```
Lane 1: 1 kb+ Ladder
Lane 2: Col-0 (WT control) — expected ~900 bp WT band only
Lane 3: SALK_045678 plant #1 — WT + T-DNA bands = HETEROZYGOUS
Lane 4: SALK_045678 plant #2 — T-DNA band only (~600 bp) = HOMOZYGOUS
Lane 5: SALK_045678 plant #3 — WT band only = WILD-TYPE
Lane 6: NTC (no template control) — no bands (good, no contamination)
```

## Common Issues

| Issue | Cause | Fix |
|-------|-------|-----|
| No visible bands | Staining failed, gel ran too long, or no DNA in sample | Check staining. Re-stain if needed. Check PCR. |
| Bands too faint | Low DNA concentration or short staining time | Load more sample next time, or stain longer |
| Bands smeared (not sharp) | DNA degraded, overloaded, or gel ran too hot | Use fresh DNA, load less, run at lower voltage |
| Photo is blurry | Camera movement | Stabilize phone, use timer/burst mode |

## Documentation

The annotated gel image IS the documentation. Every gel you run produces a labeled, saved image in your lab notebook. No exceptions.
