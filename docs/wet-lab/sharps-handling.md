# Sharps Handling and Disposal

## Resources

**Equipment:** Sharps containers (red), broken glass boxes

**Related Protocols:** [[lab-orientation]], [[breakage-response]], [[waste-disposal-quick-reference]]

**Purpose:** What counts as a sharp, where the containers are, and how to dispose properly.

## Time estimate

**Reading time:** ~5 min

---

## What Counts as a Sharp

| Sharp | Examples |
|-------|---------|
| **Needles and syringes** | Syringe needles (if used for any purpose), filter needles |
| **Razor blades and scalpels** | Single-edge blades for tissue sectioning, scalpel blades |
| **Broken glass** | Broken beakers, flasks, pipettes, slides |
| **Pasteur pipettes** | Glass Pasteur pipettes (intact or broken) |

**Pipette tips are NOT sharps** (unless contaminated with biohazardous material, in which case they go in biohazard waste).

## Disposal

### Needles and blades: Red sharps container

1. Do not recap needles. Drop them directly into the sharps container.
2. Used razor blades: drop into the sharps container.
3. Never push items down into the container with your hand.
4. When the container is 3/4 full, close it and request a replacement through EH&S or Grey.

### Broken glass: Broken glass box

1. Broken glassware goes in the designated **broken glass box** (usually a lined cardboard box labeled "BROKEN GLASS").
2. This is separate from the sharps container and separate from regular trash.
3. When the box is full, tape it shut, label it "BROKEN GLASS," and arrange for pickup or disposal through EH&S.

### What does NOT go in sharps containers

- Plastic pipette tips (regular trash or biohazard waste)
- Gloves (regular trash)
- Paper towels (regular trash)
- Intact glassware you no longer need (clean it and return it, or ask about surplus)

## Documentation

No separate lab notebook entry needed. But if you sustain a sharps injury (needle stick, cut from a blade or glass), report it to Grey and UC Davis Occupational Health.
