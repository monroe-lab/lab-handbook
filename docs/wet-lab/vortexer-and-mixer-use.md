# Vortexer and Mixer Use

## Resources

**Equipment:** [[vortex-mixer]] (benchtop), [[tube-rotator]] (hula mixer, if available)

**Location:** [[bench]] in [[room-robbins-0170]]

**Purpose:** Learn when to vortex, when to flick, and when to invert. Different mixing methods suit different situations, and using the wrong one can ruin a sample.

## Time estimate

**Wall time:** ~15 min | **Hands-on:** 15 min

## Mixing Methods

### Vortex

A vortex mixer vibrates rapidly to create a whirlpool inside the tube.

- **When to use:** Resuspending pellets, mixing buffers, dissolving salts, mixing reagents before use.
- **When NOT to use:** High-molecular-weight (HMW) DNA. Vortexing shears long DNA fragments into short ones. If a protocol involves HMW DNA, it will explicitly say "do not vortex." Use gentle inversion instead.
- **How:** Hold the tube against the rubber cup at the top of the vortexer. Press down (or use the touch mode). 3-5 seconds is usually enough.
- **Speed:** Most vortexers have a speed dial. Medium speed is fine for routine mixing. Max speed for stubborn pellets.

### Flick

Flick the bottom of the tube with your finger to mix the contents.

- **When to use:** Quick mixing of small volumes in PCR tubes or microcentrifuge tubes. Mixing after adding a small-volume reagent (like an enzyme) to a larger volume.
- **How:** Hold the tube near the top between your thumb and forefinger. Flick the bottom sharply with your other hand. Then do a pulse spin to collect the liquid at the bottom.

### Invert

Turn the tube upside down and back, gently.

- **When to use:** Mixing HMW DNA, mixing viscous solutions, any time a protocol says "mix gently."
- **How:** Close the cap. Turn the tube upside down and back. Repeat 5-10 times. Do not shake.
- **[[tube-rotator|Tube rotator / hula mixer]]:** For extended gentle mixing (e.g., "rotate for 10 minutes"), place tubes on the rotator. This is common in extraction protocols.

### Pipette mixing

Aspirate and dispense repeatedly with a pipette.

- **When to use:** Mixing in wells of a plate, mixing small volumes where a vortex would splash liquid onto the cap, resuspending beads on a magnetic rack.
- **How:** Set the pipette to ~80% of the total volume. Pipette up and down 5-10 times. Avoid introducing bubbles.

## Quick Reference

| Situation | Method |
|-----------|--------|
| Resuspend a pellet | Vortex or pipette mix |
| Mix a PCR master mix | Vortex briefly, then pulse spin |
| Add enzyme to a reaction | Flick, then pulse spin |
| Mix HMW DNA | Invert gently. Never vortex. |
| Mix beads (SPRI, [[magnetic-rack|magnetic]]) | Vortex the stock tube. Pipette mix after adding to sample. |
| Dissolve salt in buffer | Vortex or stir |
