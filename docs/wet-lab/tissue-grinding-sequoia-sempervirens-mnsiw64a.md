## Purpose

Mechanically disrupt plant leaf tissue using liquid nitrogen and mortar/pestle to produce fine powder suitable for DNA extraction. Optimized for tough, fibrous tissue like Sequoia sempervirens.

## Materials

* Liquid nitrogen (fill dewar 30 min before use)
* Mortar and pestle (autoclaved, pre-chilled at -80C)
* Fresh leaf tissue (\~100mg per sample)
* Stainless steel spatula (pre-chilled)
* 1.5mL microcentrifuge tubes (pre-labeled, pre-chilled)
* Analytical balance

## Procedure

1. Pre-chill mortar, pestle, and spatula in liquid nitrogen for 5 minutes
2. Weigh \~100mg fresh leaf tissue on analytical balance
3. Place tissue in mortar. Add liquid nitrogen to cover tissue completely.
4. Grind tissue with firm, circular motions. Redwood tissue is fibrous and requires 3-4 rounds of grinding with LN2 replenishment.
5. Continue adding LN2 as it evaporates. Tissue should become a fine white/green powder.
6. Transfer powder to pre-labeled 1.5mL tube using chilled spatula. Do NOT let powder thaw.
7. Proceed immediately to DNA extraction OR snap-freeze and store at -80C.

## Safety

* Wear cryogenic gloves and face shield when handling LN2
* Work in well-ventilated area (LN2 displaces O2)
* Never seal containers of LN2
