# Harvesting Leaf Tissue

## Resources

**Equipment:** Fine scissors or razor blade, forceps, [[liquid-nitrogen-dewar]], cryo-safe [[microtube]] (1.5 mL or 2 mL), freezer box

**Supplies:** [[cryo-labels]] or cryo marker, aluminum foil, ice bucket

**Reagents:** [[liquid-nitrogen]], [[ethanol-70]]

**Storage:** [[freezer-minus80-a]]

**Related Protocols:** [[liquid-nitrogen-safety]], [[tube-and-sample-labeling]], [[quick-dna-extraction]], [[qiagen-dneasy-extraction]]

**Prerequisites:** [[liquid-nitrogen-safety]], plants with true leaves (~3 weeks from planting)

**Purpose:** Collect leaf tissue from Arabidopsis plants and flash-freeze it in liquid nitrogen for DNA (or RNA) extraction. Proper tissue collection and immediate freezing preserves nucleic acid quality.

## Time estimate

**Wall time:** ~1-1.5 hr (including setup and cleanup) | **Hands-on:** ~1 hr

---

## Before You Start

Check that you have:

- [[liquid-nitrogen]] in a small [[liquid-nitrogen-dewar|dewar]] (see [[liquid-nitrogen-safety]] for handling)
- Pre-labeled cryo-safe tubes (label BEFORE harvesting; you cannot write on frozen tubes)
- Clean scissors or a razor blade
- Forceps
- A freezer box with available slots in the -80C freezer

**PPE for liquid nitrogen:** Safety glasses, cryo gloves (when pouring LN2), lab coat. See [[liquid-nitrogen-safety]].

## How Much Tissue

| Downstream use | Amount needed | What that looks like |
|---------------|--------------|---------------------|
| Quick DNA extraction (Buffer A) | 1-2 small leaves | Clip from the rosette, ~10-20 mg |
| Kit extraction (DNeasy) | 1-2 leaves | ~50-100 mg |
| HMW DNA extraction | 3-5 leaves or more | ~100-200 mg |

When in doubt, collect more than you think you need. You can always use less, but you cannot go back to the same plant after it's been moved to the freezer.

## Procedure

### 1. Set up

1. Fill a small dewar with liquid nitrogen. Place it on your bench.
2. Pre-label your tubes. See [[tube-and-sample-labeling]] for what goes on each label.
3. Open the tube caps and place tubes in a rack next to the dewar.
4. Have scissors/razor and forceps clean and ready.

### 2. Harvest

1. Identify the plant and leaf you will sample. For rosette-stage Arabidopsis, use **young, fully expanded leaves** (not the oldest, yellowing leaves and not the newest, tiny ones).
2. Using scissors or a razor blade, cut the leaf at the petiole (where the leaf meets the stem).
3. Using forceps, place the leaf directly into the labeled tube. Work quickly; the goal is to minimize time between cutting and freezing.
4. Immediately plunge the open tube into liquid nitrogen. The tissue flash-freezes in seconds.
5. Remove the tube from the LN2 with forceps or cryo gloves. Cap it while still cold.
6. Place the capped tube in the freezer box on dry ice or immediately into the -80C freezer.

### 3. Between samples

- If sampling multiple genotypes: **wipe scissors with [[ethanol-70|70% ethanol]]** between plants to prevent cross-contamination. For critical genotyping, use a fresh blade for each sample.
- If sampling multiple plants of the same genotype: ethanol wipe is less critical but still good practice.

### 4. Storage

1. Place all tubes into a freezer box.
2. Record the box grid positions in the [inventory system](../inventory-app/index.html).
3. Store at **[[freezer-minus80-a|-80C]]**. Tissue is stable at -80C for years.

## Important Notes

- **Do not let tissue thaw.** Once frozen, tissue must stay frozen until extraction. Thawing activates nucleases that degrade DNA and RNA. If a tube thaws accidentally, extract immediately or discard.
- **Do not overfill tubes.** Leave room for the tissue to be ground during extraction. About 1/3 full is maximum.
- **Tissue from plates vs. soil:** Both work. For plate-grown seedlings, use sterile forceps to remove the seedling from the agar, blot excess agar on a paper towel, then flash-freeze. Some agar carryover is acceptable.

## Common Errors

| Error | Consequence | How to avoid |
|-------|------------|--------------|
| Forgetting to pre-label tubes | Scrambling to label frozen tubes, identity errors | Always label before harvesting |
| Not freezing quickly enough | Nucleic acid degradation | Work next to the LN2 dewar, plunge immediately |
| Cross-contaminating between genotypes | Wrong DNA in your extraction | Clean scissors between plants |
| Thawing and re-freezing | Degraded DNA/RNA | Keep tubes on dry ice or in LN2 until -80C storage |
| Harvesting old/yellowing leaves | Lower quality DNA, less tissue | Use young, fully expanded leaves |
