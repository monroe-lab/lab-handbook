# Watering and Plant Care

## Resources

**Equipment:** Watering can or squeeze bottle, spray bottle, growth chamber access

**Related Protocols:** [[planting-in-soil]], [[transferring-seedlings-to-soil]], [[growth-chamber-operation]], [[harvesting-leaf-tissue]], [[bulking-seed]]

**Prerequisites:** [[planting-in-soil]] or [[transferring-seedlings-to-soil]]

**Purpose:** Keep your plants alive and healthy from planting through seed harvest. This protocol covers the full lifecycle of Arabidopsis plant care in the lab.

## Time estimate

**Wall time:** ~30 min to read | **Ongoing:** ~15-30 min per visit, 2-3 times per week

---

## Watering Schedule

| Growth stage | Frequency | Method |
|-------------|-----------|--------|
| Seedlings (first 2 weeks) | Every 1-2 days | Bottom-water (fill the tray, not the pots) or mist from above |
| Rosette stage (weeks 2-5) | Every 2-3 days | Bottom-water |
| Bolting/flowering (weeks 5-8) | Every 2-3 days | Bottom-water. Plants drink more as they flower |
| Seed maturation (weeks 8-10+) | Reduce gradually | Let soil dry between waterings. Stop watering entirely once siliques are brown |

### How to bottom-water

1. Pour water into the tray that holds the pots (not directly onto the soil surface).
2. Let the soil wick water up from below for 15-30 minutes.
3. Drain any standing water after 30 minutes. Do not leave pots sitting in water permanently; this promotes root rot and fungal growth.

### How much water

- The soil should be evenly moist but not soggy.
- If the surface looks dry and the pot feels light, it needs water.
- If the surface is dark and the pot feels heavy, skip watering.
- Overwatering is worse than underwatering for Arabidopsis.

## What to Look For

Check your plants at every watering visit.

### Healthy growth

- Rosette leaves are flat and green
- New leaves emerging from the center
- Bolting stem elongating (starts ~4 weeks)
- White flowers opening at the stem tip
- Siliques (seed pods) forming along the stem

### Problems

| Symptom | Likely cause | Action |
|---------|-------------|--------|
| Wilting, drooping leaves | Underwatered | Water immediately. Plants usually recover within hours |
| Yellow lower leaves | Overwatering, nutrient deficiency, or normal senescence (old leaves) | Reduce watering frequency. If persistent, ask Grey |
| White fuzzy growth on soil | Fungal growth (common, usually harmless) | Improve air circulation, reduce watering. Scrape off if heavy |
| Aphids (tiny green/white insects on leaves) | Pest infestation | Report to Grey immediately. May need treatment |
| Damping off (seedlings collapse at soil line) | Fungal disease from overwatering | Remove affected pots. Reduce watering for remaining plants |
| No bolting after 5 weeks | Short-day conditions, or slow ecotype | Verify the growth chamber is set to 16h light. Some ecotypes are slower |
| Purple leaves | Phosphorus deficiency or cold stress | Check temperature. Usually cosmetic and not harmful |

## Seed Maturation and Drying

When you are growing plants to harvest seed (see [[bulking-seed]]):

1. After flowering, siliques develop along the stem. They start green and turn tan/brown as seeds mature.
2. **Stop watering** when most siliques are brown and the plant begins to look dry and straw-colored.
3. Let the plant dry completely in the growth chamber for 1-2 weeks.
4. Harvest seed only when siliques are fully dry and brittle. See [[bulking-seed]].
