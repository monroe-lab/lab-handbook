# Bulking Seed from Arabidopsis

## Resources

**Equipment:** Paper envelopes or coin envelopes, fine-mesh sieve (optional), clean bench surface

**Supplies:** Labeling supplies (see [[tube-and-sample-labeling]])

**Storage:** [[fridge-4c-main]] (long-term with desiccant)

**Related Protocols:** [[watering-and-plant-care]], [[planting-in-soil]], [[growth-chamber-operation]], [[seed-sterilization]]

**Prerequisites:** Arabidopsis plants that have flowered and produced mature siliques (typically 8-10 weeks from planting)

**Purpose:** Harvest, clean, and store seed from Arabidopsis plants. This creates a renewable seed stock of your genotype. For the capstone project, you will bulk seed from your assigned SALK T-DNA line to create a master stock for the lab.

## Time estimate

**Wall time:** ~1 hr for harvest + cleanup | **Calendar time:** 8-10 weeks of plant growth before harvest

---

## Before You Start

Your plants must be at the correct stage:

- Most siliques (seed pods) on the main stem are **tan or brown** (not green)
- The plant looks dry and straw-colored
- You stopped watering at least 1-2 weeks ago (see [[watering-and-plant-care]])
- Siliques are **brittle** and crack open when you touch them

If siliques are still green, wait. Harvesting immature seed gives you low-quality or non-viable seed.

## Procedure

### 1. Harvest

1. Bring the dried plant (in its pot) to a clean bench surface. Lay down a sheet of clean white paper to catch loose seeds.
2. Hold the plant over the paper.
3. Gently roll each silique between your fingers. Mature siliques will crack open and release seeds onto the paper.
4. Work from the top of the stem downward. The newest (topmost) siliques may still be slightly green; skip those.
5. For stubborn siliques, gently crush them between your fingers over the paper.
6. Collect all released seeds on the paper.

### 2. Clean

Seeds will be mixed with chaff (silique fragments, stem pieces, dust).

1. Tilt the paper and gently tap it. Seeds will roll; chaff generally won't.
2. For larger batches: pass the material through a fine-mesh sieve. Seeds fall through; chaff stays behind.
3. Blow gently across the paper to remove lightweight chaff without losing seeds.
4. You do not need perfectly clean seed for lab use. A few bits of chaff are fine.

### 3. Store

1. Transfer clean seed into a labeled **paper envelope** or **coin envelope**. Do not use plastic bags (seeds need to stay dry; plastic traps moisture and promotes fungal growth).
2. Label the envelope with:
   - Genotype / line name (e.g., `Col-0`, `SALK_045678 msh2-1`)
   - Date harvested
   - Your initials
   - Parent plant info (which plant this seed came from)
   - Generation (e.g., `M3`, `F2`, or just `bulk` for maintenance stocks)
3. Store envelopes in a cool, dry place. Room temperature is fine for short-term (months). For long-term storage (years), store at 4C in a sealed container with desiccant.

### 4. Log the seed stock

Log the new seed stock in the [inventory system](../inventory-app/index.html):

- Item name (genotype)
- Quantity (approximate number of seeds: handful = ~200-500, full envelope = ~1000+)
- Storage location
- Date
- Your initials

## Yield Expectations

A healthy Col-0 plant produces ~5,000-10,000 seeds. Mutant lines may produce less, especially if the mutation affects fertility.

For the capstone, you need enough seed to:
- Maintain a master stock for the lab (keep most of the harvest)
- Use a small amount for your own genotyping and extraction
