# Sodium arsenate dibasic heptahydrate

> ℹ️ **Chemistry**
> Na₂HAsO₄·7H₂O; arsenate (AsO₄³⁻) is a structural mimic of phosphate that competes for phosphate transporters and uncouples phosphate-dependent metabolism.

> 💡 **Lab use**
> Tool reagent for arsenate-tolerance and phosphate-uptake studies in plants and microbes; useful precisely because cells can't tell it apart from phosphate.

> ⚠️ **Safety**
> Highly toxic and a known carcinogen — handle in a hood, wear gloves, label all dilutions, and dispose as hazardous waste.
