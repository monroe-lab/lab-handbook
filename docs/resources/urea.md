# Urea

> ℹ️ **Chemistry**
> CO(NH₂)₂, a small uncharged molecule that disrupts hydrogen bonds; at high concentrations (6–8 M) it unfolds proteins and melts nucleic-acid secondary structure.

> 💡 **Lab use**
> Denaturant in protein extraction buffers and SDS-PAGE sample prep, and the standard denaturant in urea-PAGE for resolving small RNAs and ssDNA at single-nucleotide resolution.

> ⚠️ **Safety**
> Low hazard; avoid heating concentrated solutions because urea decomposes to ammonia and isocyanate, which can carbamylate proteins.
