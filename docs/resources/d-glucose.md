# D-(+)-Glucose

> ℹ️ **Chemistry**
> The D-enantiomer of the six-carbon aldose sugar (C₆H₁₂O₆); the universal carbon source for most heterotrophic life and the substrate for glycolysis.

> 💡 **Lab use**
> Standard carbon source in microbial growth media (LB-glucose, M9, minimal media) and in plant tissue culture as an alternative to sucrose, especially for protoplast and embryo culture.

> ⚠️ **Safety**
> Low hazard under normal lab handling.
