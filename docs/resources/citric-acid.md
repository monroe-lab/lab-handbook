# Citric acid

> ℹ️ **Chemistry**
> A small triprotic organic acid (pKa ~3.1, 4.8, 6.4) that chelates metal ions and is the central intermediate of the TCA cycle.

> 💡 **Lab use**
> Paired with sodium citrate to make citrate buffer in the pH 3–6.5 range (e.g. SSC for nucleic acid hybridization, antigen retrieval buffers); also used to acidify plant tissue culture media and as a mild metal chelator.

> ⚠️ **Safety**
> Eye and respiratory irritant as a dust; otherwise low hazard.
