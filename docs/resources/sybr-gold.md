# SYBR Gold

## What it is

SYBR Gold is a fluorescent nucleic acid stain from Invitrogen/ThermoFisher. It binds to double-stranded DNA, single-stranded DNA, and RNA, and fluoresces bright green/yellow when excited by UV light (~300 nm) or blue light (~495 nm). It is significantly more sensitive than ethidium bromide (up to 1000x) and safer to handle.

## Why we use it

SYBR Gold is the standard gel stain in this lab. After running an agarose gel, the gel is soaked in a dilute SYBR Gold solution. The dye intercalates into the DNA, making bands visible on the [[uv-transilluminator]]. Used in [[gel-electrophoresis]] and [[gel-imaging-and-annotation]].

> ⚠️ **Handle with gloves**
> SYBR Gold is a nucleic acid stain and should be treated as a potential mutagen. Always wear [[nitrile-gloves]] when handling. Dispose of staining solution as chemical waste. See [[waste-disposal-quick-reference]].

Working solution aliquots are stored in the -20C freezer in dark containers (light-sensitive). Stock solution is 10,000x concentrate.
