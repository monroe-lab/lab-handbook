# Disodium Ethylenediamine Tetraacetate

> ℹ️ **Chemistry**
> Na₂EDTA, the disodium salt of EDTA — a hexadentate chelator that grabs divalent metal ions (Mg²⁺, Ca²⁺, Mn²⁺, Zn²⁺) from six coordination angles. The disodium form has lower pH (~5) and dissolves more readily than the free acid.

> 💡 **Lab use**
> Sequesters Mg²⁺/Ca²⁺ cofactors to inactivate nucleases; the foundation of TE buffer and a key component of TAE/TBE running buffers.

> ⚠️ **Safety**
> Eye irritant; also inhibits downstream Mg²⁺-dependent enzymes (PCR, restriction digests) if it carries through.
