# HiFi Lysis Buffer

## What it is

A detergent-based cell lysis buffer optimized for high-molecular-weight DNA extraction from plant tissue. The combination of SDS (detergent that dissolves cell membranes), high salt (denatures proteins), EDTA (chelates divalent cations to inhibit nucleases), PVP-40 (binds polyphenols that would otherwise co-purify with and damage DNA), sodium metabisulfite (antioxidant, prevents phenolic browning), and beta-mercaptoethanol (reducing agent, breaks disulfide bonds in proteins) produces a harsh lysis cocktail that liberates genomic DNA while neutralizing the abundant secondary metabolites in plant tissue.

## Why we use it

This is the lysis buffer for the [[hifi-dna-extraction|HiFi DNA Extraction]] protocol (Satoyo's method, based on Rabanal et al. 2022). It is made fresh on the day of extraction because sodium metabisulfite and beta-mercaptoethanol degrade over time. The base solution (water, Tris, EDTA, NaCl) can be pre-mixed and stored, but PVP-40, sodium metabisulfite, BME, and SDS are added fresh.

> ⚠️ **Contains hazardous reagents**
> [[2-mercaptoethanol|Beta-mercaptoethanol]] is toxic and has an extremely strong odor. [[sodium-dodecyl-sulfate|SDS]] is an irritant. Prepare this buffer in the [[fume-hood]]. Wear [[nitrile-gloves]] and safety glasses.

## Recipe (10 mL)

| Reagent | Stock | Amount | Final concentration |
|---------|-------|--------|-------------------|
| PVP-40 | powder | 0.1 g | 1% (w/v) |
| Sodium metabisulfite | powder | 0.1 g | 1% (w/v) |
| NaCl | 5 M | 1 mL | 0.5 M |
| Tris-HCl pH 8 | 1 M | 1 mL | 100 mM |
| EDTA pH 8 | 0.5 M | 1 mL | 50 mM |
| Beta-mercaptoethanol | neat | 200 uL | 2% (v/v) |
| SDS | 10% | 1.5 mL | 1.5% |
| Molecular biology grade water | | to 10 mL | |

**Add SDS last** (it precipitates at lower temperatures if mixed first).

## Storage

**Do not store.** Make fresh on the day of extraction. The base solution (water + Tris + EDTA + NaCl only) can be pre-mixed and stored at room temperature for months.
