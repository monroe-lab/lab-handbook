# Ammonium acetate

> ℹ️ **Chemistry**
> NH₄⁺ CH₃COO⁻, a highly water-soluble salt of a weak acid and a weak base. Both ions are volatile, so the salt sublimes/evaporates cleanly under vacuum.

> 💡 **Lab use**
> Used in DNA/RNA precipitation as a milder alternative to sodium acetate (especially for removing free dNTPs and primers), and as a "volatile" buffer for mass spectrometry where leftover salt would interfere.

> ⚠️ **Safety**
> Low hazard under normal lab handling; mild irritant if inhaled as dust.
