# Gaseous hydrochloric acid

> ℹ️ **Chemistry**
> HCl in its anhydrous gas form; dissolves in water to give hydrochloric acid, a strong monoprotic acid that fully dissociates into H⁺ and Cl⁻.

> 💡 **Lab use**
> Used for vapor-phase sterilization and for low-water acid hydrolysis (e.g. peptide hydrolysis for amino acid analysis) where adding aqueous HCl would dilute the sample.

> ⚠️ **Safety**
> Severe respiratory and eye hazard, corrosive — only handle in a fume hood, and never open a cylinder without proper regulators and PPE.
