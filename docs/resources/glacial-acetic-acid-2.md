# Glacial Acetic Acid

> ℹ️ **Chemistry**
> CH₃COOH in essentially pure form (>99%), a weak organic acid (pKa 4.76) that freezes into ice-like crystals just below room temperature — hence "glacial."

> 💡 **Lab use**
> The "A" in TAE buffer for DNA gels, and used to titrate Tris-based buffers down to neutral or acidic pH; also a common fixative component (e.g. Carnoy's solution) for cytology.

> ⚠️ **Safety**
> Corrosive to skin and eyes, vapors irritate the airways — dispense in a fume hood and store away from oxidizers.
