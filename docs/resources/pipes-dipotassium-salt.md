# PIPES, dipotassium salt

> ℹ️ **Chemistry**
> Dipotassium salt of PIPES (piperazine-N,N'-bis(2-ethanesulfonic acid)); a Good's buffer with pKa ~6.8 that dissolves faster than the free acid because the sulfonates are already deprotonated.

> 💡 **Lab use**
> Drop-in PIPES buffer when you want quick dissolution and don't mind adding K⁺ to your final solution; common in fixation buffers and cytoskeleton work.

> ⚠️ **Safety**
> Low hazard under normal lab handling.
