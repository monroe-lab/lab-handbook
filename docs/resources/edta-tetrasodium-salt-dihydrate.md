# EDTA tetrasodium salt dihydrate

> ℹ️ **Chemistry**
> Na₄EDTA·2H₂O, the fully deprotonated tetrasodium salt of EDTA — same hexadentate metal-chelating core, but dissolves in water with an alkaline pH (~11) instead of needing a separate pH adjustment.

> 💡 **Lab use**
> Convenient when you want EDTA chelation without lowering pH; useful in alkaline lysis prep, in some plant DNA extraction buffers, and anywhere the disodium salt is too acidic.

> ⚠️ **Safety**
> Eye and skin irritant; basic enough that solutions can sting cuts.
