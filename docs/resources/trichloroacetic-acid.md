# Trichloroacetic acid

> ℹ️ **Chemistry**
> CCl₃COOH (TCA), an organochlorine carboxylic acid orders of magnitude stronger than acetic acid because three chlorines pull electron density off the carboxyl group.

> 💡 **Lab use**
> Standard protein precipitant — adding 10–20% TCA crashes proteins out of solution while leaving small molecules behind, useful for concentrating dilute samples and stopping enzymatic reactions cold.

> ⚠️ **Safety**
> Severely corrosive; causes deep skin and eye burns. Always handle in a fume hood with full PPE.
