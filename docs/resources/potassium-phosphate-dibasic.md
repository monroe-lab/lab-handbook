# Potassium phosphate dibasic

> ℹ️ **Chemistry**
> K₂HPO₄, the basic component of a potassium phosphate buffer system; deprotonation/protonation between HPO₄²⁻ and H₂PO₄⁻ buffers around pKa ~7.2.

> 💡 **Lab use**
> Mixed with the monobasic salt (KH₂PO₄) in tunable ratios to make potassium phosphate buffers for enzyme assays, protein chromatography, and physiological buffers where Na⁺ is undesirable.

> ⚠️ **Safety**
> Low hazard.
