# Liquid Nitrogen

## What it is

Nitrogen gas cooled to -196C, at which point it becomes a liquid. Colorless, odorless. Boils immediately at room temperature, producing large volumes of nitrogen gas.

## Why we use it

Flash-freezing tissue samples for DNA/RNA extraction. Rapid freezing preserves nucleic acid quality by instantly inactivating degradative enzymes (nucleases). Also used for grinding tissue with mortar and pestle.

> ⚠️ **Cryogenic hazard**
> Can cause severe frostbite on skin contact. Displaces oxygen in enclosed spaces, creating asphyxiation risk. Always use in well-ventilated areas. Full PPE required: cryo gloves, face shield, safety glasses, lab coat, closed-toe shoes. See [[liquid-nitrogen-safety]] and [[cryogens-sop]].
