# alpha-D-Glucose

> ℹ️ **Chemistry**
> The α-anomer of D-glucose (C₆H₁₂O₆), the most abundant monosaccharide in biology. In water it slowly mutarotates to a mix of α and β pyranose forms.

> 💡 **Lab use**
> Carbon source in microbial growth media (e.g. YPD, M9 + glucose) and in plant tissue culture media as a supplement or sucrose alternative.

> ⚠️ **Safety**
> Low hazard under normal lab handling.
