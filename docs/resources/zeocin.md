# Zeocin

> ℹ️ **Chemistry**
> A copper-chelated glycopeptide of the bleomycin family; the copper-bound form is inactive, and once it enters cells the copper is released and the drug intercalates and cleaves DNA.

> 💡 **Lab use**
> Selection antibiotic for the Sh ble resistance marker, used in bacteria, yeast, plant, and mammalian cells (one of the few selection drugs that works across all of them); typical working range 25–500 μg/mL depending on the host.

> ⚠️ **Safety**
> Mutagen and suspected carcinogen; light- and heat-sensitive. Store at 4°C in the dark, handle with gloves, and avoid aerosols.
