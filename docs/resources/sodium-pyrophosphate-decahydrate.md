# Sodium pyrophosphate decahydrate

> ℹ️ **Chemistry**
> Na₄P₂O₇·10H₂O, two phosphate groups joined by a P–O–P bridge; mimics ATP/ADP phosphate geometry, which is why phosphatases recognize it.

> 💡 **Lab use**
> Phosphatase inhibitor in protein lysis buffers (preserves phosphorylation state for Western blots and kinase assays); also a mild chelator and dispersing agent.

> ⚠️ **Safety**
> Low hazard; mild irritant to eyes and skin.
