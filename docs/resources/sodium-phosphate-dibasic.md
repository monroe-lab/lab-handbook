# Sodium phosphate dibasic

> ℹ️ **Chemistry**
> Na₂HPO₄, the disodium salt of phosphoric acid; in water it gives a mildly basic solution and pairs with monobasic NaH₂PO₄ to form a phosphate buffer with pKa ~7.2.

> 💡 **Lab use**
> The basic half of sodium phosphate buffer (PB and PBS), which is the standard near-neutral buffer for protein work, antibody storage, and cell-friendly washes where Tris is unsuitable.

> ⚠️ **Safety**
> Low hazard; mild eye and skin irritant as a dust.
