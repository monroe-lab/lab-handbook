# Diethyl pyrocarbonate

> ℹ️ **Chemistry**
> DEPC, a reactive carbonate ester that covalently modifies histidine residues (and amines) in proteins, irreversibly inactivating most enzymes; hydrolyzes in water to ethanol and CO₂.

> 💡 **Lab use**
> Added to water (typically 0.1%) and autoclaved to inactivate RNases, producing the "DEPC-treated water" used throughout RNA work; the autoclaving step destroys residual DEPC so it doesn't carbamoylate your RNA.

> ⚠️ **Safety**
> Suspected carcinogen and severe respiratory irritant; only handle in a fume hood and never use with Tris buffers (it reacts with primary amines).
