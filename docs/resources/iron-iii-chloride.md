# Iron(III) chloride

> ℹ️ **Chemistry**
> FeCl₃, a Lewis-acidic ferric salt that hydrolyzes in water to give acidic, brown-yellow solutions and binds strongly to phosphate, hydroxide, and many organics.

> 💡 **Lab use**
> Used in stain solutions and histochemistry (e.g. Perls' / Prussian blue iron stain when paired with ferrocyanide), and as an iron source in trace-element supplements for plant or microbial growth media.

> ⚠️ **Safety**
> Corrosive — irritates skin, eyes, and airways and the solid is hygroscopic; handle with gloves and avoid contact with metals (it etches stainless steel).
