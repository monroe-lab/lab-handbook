# DNAse

> ℹ️ **Chemistry**
> An endonuclease (typically DNase I, ~31 kDa from bovine pancreas) that hydrolyzes phosphodiester bonds in double- and single-stranded DNA, producing short oligonucleotides; requires Mg²⁺ or Mn²⁺ as a cofactor.

> 💡 **Lab use**
> The standard way to remove genomic DNA contamination from RNA preps before RT-qPCR or RNA-seq; also used in chromatin work (DNase-seq footprinting) and in nick translation labeling.

> ⚠️ **Safety**
> Low hazard chemically, but a potential respiratory sensitizer; the bigger lab risk is cross-contaminating downstream experiments — keep dedicated tips and tubes.
